# Tidal Energy

The regular movement of the waves could become a source of electricity

by Elizabeth Koroma

## Article

As the quest for new, environmentally friendly energy sources continues, some people are proposing that we harness the regular motion of the tides. While this energy source has limits, it also has potential as a clean and reliable energy source.

As anyone who lives near the ocean knows, the waves reach different heights at different times of day. This is because the moon's gravity pulls on the water, but as the moon moves around the Earth, the areas of the planet where this force is strongest change as well.

Tides change every day, which means that if people were to harness the energy of this moving water, they would have a consistent, reliable energy source. This can be accomplished by installing tidal turbines on the ocean floor. As the water moves when the tide goes in or out, the water turns the turbine, which is connected to a generator.

Another approach to tidal energy takes advantage of the height difference between the water at high tide and the water at low tide. This typically involves building a tidal barrage, which is a barrier that traps a large amount of water when the tide is in. Then when the tide goes out, the water trapped in the barrage flows out, turning turbines that are inside the barrage and generating electricity.

Tidal energy has some notable advantages. It is renewable energy, which means it will never run out. It does not require fuel or produce waste, and unlike wind power, it is not dependent upon the weather.

However, tidal power also has some weaknesses. Tidal power plants are very expensive and take a long time to build. They also can only be constructed in specific locations: places on the coastline where the water is deep enough and the current is strong enough. Perhaps most concerning, since environmental issues motivate the push for tidal energy, tidal power plants themselves can damage the environment. They can change the way water flows and the water quality, which has a ripple effect on other aspects of the ecosystem.

Animals can be injured by the rotating turbines, and the sound produced by tidal power plants may interfere with some creatures' ability to navigate. These problems raise questions about whether it is better to build tidal power plants than to use other energy sources.

Due to the constraints on where tidal power plants can be built, they are unlikely to ever become the world's main power source. Nevertheless, tidal power plants could be a useful power source among others as the world searches for more renewable energy sources.

## Key Points

- People are developing ways to use the tides to generate electricity in different ways.
- Tidal power is clean and consistent, but it is limited and may harm marine life.

## Usage Tip

### Tip 1
As N + knows, S + V

句型用來表示「如某人所知……」。用來引出眾所周知的事實或一般性觀察。例如：As everyone knows, honesty is the best policy. 眾所周知，誠實是最好的策略。

### Tip 2
It is better to + V1 than (to) + V2

這個句型用來表達「與其……不如……」。than 後的 to + V2 則可省略。例如：It is better to plan your tasks carefully than (to) rush into execution without thinking. 與其不要考慮就倉促執行，不如仔細規劃好任務。
