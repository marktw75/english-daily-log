# Earth Day

A day to remind us that small actions can make a big difference

by Heather Lin

## Article

In today's world, people take steps to protect and care for the planet. They recycle, save energy and combat climate change. But before Earth Day began in 1970, few people thought about the environment at all. At that time, the United States had a minimal number of laws to protect air and water. Factories and cars could pollute freely, and little was done to stop it.

It was not until he witnessed a massive oil spill in California in 1969 that U.S. Senator Gaylord Nelson felt he had to act. He wanted to raise awareness and inspire people to preserve the environment.

On the first Earth Day, April 22, 1970, millions of people across the U.S. attended lectures, demonstrated and took part in cleanups. They gathered in public spaces to demand action from their communities. The day got major media attention and helped bring environmental issues to light for the first time.

Since then, Earth Day has expanded its focus, and in 1990, it became a global movement. Today, over a billion people in more than 190 countries celebrate Earth Day. They share one goal: to make the Earth a cleaner, healthier place to live.

## Key Point

- Earth Day inspired people to care for the environment, which continues to face massive oil spills and other threats today.

## Usage Tip

### Tip 1
few + plural noun + V + at all

句型用來表達「幾乎沒有……」。few 本身表示「幾乎沒有」，搭配 at all 能再強調「根本沒有」的語氣。例句：Few students show interest in classical music at all these days. 如今的學生中，根本很少有人對古典樂感興趣。
