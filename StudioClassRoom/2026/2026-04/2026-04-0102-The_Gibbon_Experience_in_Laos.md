# The Gibbon Experience - Laos

A childhood fantasy with a serious purpose

by Pamela Osment

## Article

Imagine soaring over the lush rainforest on a zipline in western Laos toward your destination, one of the world's tallest treehouses. No, it's not a childhood dream. It's a reality at the Gibbon Experience in Laos.

The project took root in the mind of Jef Reumaux, the founder of the Gibbon Experience, in 1996. While hiking in the area, Reumaux photographed black crested gibbons, a critically endangered species. He felt burdened by the need to find a way to protect the rainforest's vital ecosystem.

From illegal logging and hunting to fish bombing and land schemes, there were many problems, but no funds. Then Reumaux had the idea of creating trails, treehouses and zipline networks in the forest as "a way to bring people and money to the forest."

Not until 2004 did the first treehouses and ziplines open, and when they did, people flocked to the forest. The area was then designated the Nam Kan National Park in 2008. Today, the Gibbon Experience is a thriving tourism-based conservation program that employs more than 80 full-time staff, mostly from neighboring villages. The staff includes local guides, chefs and support people who contribute to both the environmental protection and economic development of the area.

The Gibbon Experience, famous for its incredible treehouses and ziplines, draws tourists from around the world. Guests are taken to a jungle hut, located on the ground, for safety instructions and to practice with the zipline equipment. They're then ready to fly high above the canopy and gaze at the rainforest below before arriving at one of the multilevel treehouses.

These wonders of engineering are built at canopy level, 30 to 40 meters above the ground. They often include several platforms with open-air bedrooms, a bathroom and a kitchen. Hot meals are prepared by local cooks and delivered by zipline since fires aren't allowed.

Basic but comfortable, the treehouses provide the perfect view of nature in all its glory. Though gibbon sightings aren't guaranteed, those who are patient will often see them or hear them singing to one another as they swing through the forest.

The revenue from the tours goes to salaries, operations and maintenance, local aid projects, general expenses and reinvestment in the project.

Remarkable as the Gibbon Experience is, it's more than just an adventure. It's a way to help protect the Nam Kan National Park and its incredible wildlife.

## What is a gibbon?

Gibbons, found mostly in Southeast Asia, are the smallest of apes. Their extra-long arms allow them to swing easily between trees, and their loud voices help them communicate with one another, claim territory and strengthen social ties. Gibbons often walk through the trees on two legs, holding their arms high above their heads for balance.

## Usage Tip

### Tip 1
S + feel + adj./p.p. + by + N/V-ing

句型是表達某人因為某事而感到...。動詞 feel 後面可接形容詞，如 happy, sad, nervous 等，用來描述主詞的情緒、感覺或狀態；若要強調被動的感覺，則可搭配過去分詞，如 moved, encouraged, inspired 等。後面加介系詞 by 表示「因為；由於」，可接名詞或動名詞，說明造成這種感覺的原因或方式。例句：She felt moved by the kindness of strangers. 她因陌生人的善意而受到感動。

### Tip 2
Adj./Adv. as S + V, ...

意思是「雖然...但是...」，是一種倒裝結構，主要用於引導讓步副詞子句，意思等同於 although/though，表示儘管事情為真，仍然發生另一件事。形容詞置句首的例句：Tired as he was/Although he was tired, he continued working late into the night. 雖然他很累，但他仍然工作到深夜。副詞置句首的例句：Hard as she tried, she couldn't solve the problem. 雖然她很努力，但仍無法解決這個問題。
