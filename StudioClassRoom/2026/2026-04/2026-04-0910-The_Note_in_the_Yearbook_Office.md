# The Note in the Yearbook Office

Who wrote the note?

by David Morton

## Article

Ivy wasn't snooping. She was following instructions. Ms. Park, the yearbook adviser, had asked her to return the club's camera and download the images she'd taken onto the computer. She also needed to put the club members' signed permission slips for those attending the next day's sports rally in an envelope and drop it off at the main office.

When Ivy stepped into the yearbook office, the lights were off, but the door was unlocked. After downloading the pictures, she grabbed an envelope from the top of the stack in the supply drawer. It was partly sealed. Frowning, she opened it to check if it had been used. Tucked inside was a folded sheet of paper. She unfolded the paper and froze.

"I'm sorry for everything. For watching it happen. For being too scared to say something. Some days I wish I could just disappear. Maybe that would be easier, for everyone."

Ivy sat down, her hand trembling. Dean found her there ten minutes later. "You okay?"

The student computer in the yearbook office was old and sluggish, but Dean knew how to check the recent document history. After a few dead ends, he found a file named Untitled_3.docx, three drafts, all saved under the same login.

She handed him the note. "This wasn't meant to be seen. But I'm glad I found it."

Dean read it in silence. "You think it's real?"

"I think someone's hurting. And hiding," Ivy replied.

"No name. Nothing personal. Just this weird blot of ink in the corner," observed Dean.

Ivy responded, "I need to find out who wrote this before they do something that they can't undo."

"Mae Lin," Dean said. "Didn't we just see her having a laugh fest with Sasha this morning?"

Ivy nodded slowly. "There's often more to a smile than meets the eye."

Later that day, Ivy found Mae sitting alone in the back of the auditorium, sketching in a notebook. "Hey," Ivy said gently, holding out the envelope. "This was almost sent off with the permission slips."

Mae looked up, her eyes widening. "You read it?"

"I did. And I think you wanted someone to."

Mae didn't speak right away and shuffled her feet. "I hate who I am when I'm with them," she whispered. "I laughed when Sasha made fun of Lia. I didn't want to. But if I speak up, I will become the next target."

Ivy sat down. "Then maybe it's time to stop playing by their rules."

Mae slumped down in her seat. "What if I don't know who I am without them?"

Ivy smiled and held up the note. "Looks to me like you're ready to find out."

Mae's eyes filled with tears as she took the envelope back and tucked it into her notebook. "Thank you, Ivy ..."

## Usage Tip

### Tip 1
V-ing, S + V

使用現在分詞片語，用來表示主句發生時的背景、附帶動作或原因。若前後主詞相同，便可以省略主詞。例如：Frowning, she opened it to check if it had been used. 她皺著眉，把它打開來確認是否曾被使用。

### Tip 2
a/an + N/adj.

這種用法常見於固定搭配中，例如 a laugh fest，表示「一場笑聲不斷的活動」。a fun fest 也常用來表示充滿樂趣的活動。例句：The weekend concert was a real music fest for rock fans. 那場週末音樂會對搖滾樂迷來說真是一場音樂盛會。
