# How Many Friends Do You Want?

Is there a right number of friends to have?

by Linda Ludwig

## Article

Friendships play a big role in your health and well-being. They help keep you from feeling lonely and raise your sense of connection, belonging and purpose. Some people believe it is better to have many friends, while others prefer to have a few close ones. Both choices have advantages.

When you have a few close friends, you can build more trusting relationships. These friends know you well and support you through good and bad times. You can usually share personal experiences and opinions without worrying about being judged. Additionally, there is usually less drama and fewer misunderstandings. But having just a few friends can make it harder to hear new ideas, and you may tend to only interact with those who think like you do.

On the other hand, adding new friends to your circle can make life exciting. You meet people from different backgrounds, learn new ideas and grow as a person. You have more social opportunities and can practice your social skills. However, with too many friends, it's hard to give each person enough attention, and the connections may not be as genuine.

### Peter and Lily

Peter: Hey Lily, who was the girl you were with yesterday?

Lily: That's Melody. I met her at a painting class I'm taking.

Peter: It seems as if you're always expanding your social circle.

Lily: I am! I love making new friends.

Peter: I'm happy with the few close friends I have.

Lily: Making new friends is so much fun! You are exposed to new ideas and ways of doing things.

Peter: True. But sometimes when you have too many friends, you can't be close to all of them. It's hard to know who's there for you if situations are troublesome.

Lily: I get that, but associating with a wide circle of people means you always have someone to hang out with. It's nice to have options.

Peter: Maybe, but I'd rather have a few people I can share my problems with and know they'll listen. I don't have to pretend or worry about being judged.

Lily: Close friends are a necessity, but interacting with all kinds of people helps you grow socially.

Peter: Good point. I guess it depends on whether you value depth or variety more.

Lily: Maybe it's best to have a few close friends and a wider circle for fun and learning.

## Usage Tip

### Tip 1
Some ..., while others ...

用來對比兩種不同的人、情況或做法。其中 while 的作用有讓段落銜接看起來不那麼跳躍的好處，類似於 but 或 whereas。例如：While some students prefer studying alone, others enjoy group discussions. 有些學生喜歡獨自學習，而另一些則喜歡討論課。

### Tip 2
V-ing + be + so much + N

用來表達某個動作或經驗「非常……」。V-ing 的語氣較能帶入具體感受。例句：Starting a business is so much responsibility. 創業的責任真是如此重大。
