# Good Anxiety：Harnessing the Power of the Most Misunderstood Emotion

Find calm and even strength in your anxiety

  

Anxiety is often seen as a problem. For many, anxiety means racing thoughts, sleepless nights and constant stress. But in her book Good Anxiety, neuroscientist Wendy Suzuki argues that anxiety is not only natural, but also potentially useful. Instead of trying to eliminate it completely, people can learn from anxiety and even turn it into a powerful tool for personal growth.

  

Suzuki explains that anxiety is actually our brain's way of protecting us. Thousands of years ago, anxious feelings warned our ancestors of real dangers. It helped them survive wild animals and unfriendly environments. Today, however, the same feelings seem to be triggered by work, social pressures or financial concerns. These situations rarely threaten our survival, but they still activate the same stress responses. This, Suzuki says, is what makes us feel overwhelmed by anxiety.

  

The good news is that we can retain our brains. Suzuki shares simple practices like deep breathing, short walks and gratitude journaling. These techniques calm the nervous system and help people be more thoughtful in their responses to stress. Rather than being crushed by fear, readers can learn to harness their anxious energy for greater focus and resilience.

  

So how can anxiety become "good"? Suzuki suggests it start with reframing how we view it. Instead of fighting anxious thoughts, we can listen to what they are trying to tell us. For example, anxiety before a big test might signal the need for better preparation. Nervousness before a meeting might remind us that we are invested in the outcome. By interpreting anxiety as a message and not a threat, we can turn it into motivation.

  

Suzuki also introduces the idea that "superpowers" can be hidden within anxiety. She explains that when people redirect their anxious energy, it can fuel creativity, empathy and problem-solving. In fact, many successful leaders and artists confess that their anxiety pushes them to perform at their best. When channeled correctly, the very emotion that once held us back can drive us forward.

  

Good Anxiety is a book about balance. Life will always contain stress and uncertainty, but anxiety doesn't have to be the enemy. It can be transformed into a teacher - showing us where we need to grow, reminding us of what we value and helping us live with more courage and purpose.
