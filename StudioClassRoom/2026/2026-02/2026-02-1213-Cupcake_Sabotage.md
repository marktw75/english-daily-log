# Cupcake Sabotage

Who messed with the cupcakes?

  

By the time Ivy got to her bake-sale table, the damage had been done.

  

A group of students stood around it, whispering and making faces. One girl was wiping her tongue with a napkin. Another was making accusations about the cupcake display she was pointing at.

  

Ivy pushed through the crowd and froze. The lemon cupcakes - perfectly decorated and sunny yellow - smelled wrong.

  

She leaned in, took a tentative sniff and recoiled. Garlic. She read the handwritten label: "Lemon Garlic Buttercream Cupcakes."

  

Dean appeared beside her, eyeing the tray with a raised eyebrow. "Experimental," he said. "Bold."

  

"Ridiculous," Ivy replied. "That's not the label I wrote."

  

"You're sure?"

"I used yellow card stock and a blue pen. This is printer paper and a marker."

  

Dean took a photo. "Whoever did this has a chip on their shoulder."

  

This was Owl city High's annual charity bake sale, and Ivy had taken her role seriously. Her aunt had baked the cupcakes, and Ivy had frosted and labeled them herself before stepping away to help set up.

  

She grabbed the label off the table and flipped it over, noticing a faint smudge in one coner. Inked figures?

  

In the hallway, she and Dean reviewed photos of the bake sale posters. "There," Dean said, zooming in on a flyer from the student council. "See the way the loop on the "r" curls back on itself? Ivy compared it to the label. Identical."

  

They waited until cleanup. Clara, the student council vice president, stood behind the table, wiping frosting off her blazer. Ivy noticed a small container of garlic powder poking out of her open bag - and a faint smudge of black ink on her right hand.

  

"Clara," Ivy said casually. "I didn't know you were into weird flavor combos."

Clara jumped. "What? I- I don't know what you're talking about."

  

Dean tilted his head. "Just talking about the cupcakes. You know, the ones everyone hated?"

  

Clara forced a laugh. "Please. As if I have time to mess with cupcakes? I've been up to my eyeballs running this whole event."

  

Ivy held up the fake label. "This isn't the one I made. Wrong paper, wrong pen ... and the "r" has your signature flourish."

  

Dean held up his phone, where a video played a student dramatically gagging after biting into a cupcake. "Your joke went viral. Congratulations."

  

Clara's jaw tightened. "Nobody ever buys my stuff. I work just as hard as every else, but no one even looks at my display. I just wanted people to notice."

  

Ivy glanced at the now-empty cupcake tray and raised an eyebrow. "Well, thanks to you, my cupcakes were so bad that everyone had to try one, which means we raised a lot of money. Let's call it a collaboration."

  

Clara didn't reply. She just turned and walked off, wiping her fingers again.
