# The Riches of Oman

Discover a wealth of natural and cultural wonders in this Middle Eastern country

  

Have you ever played the conversation game Would You Rather ...? In this game, someone gives you two options, and you have to pick one. For example: Would you rather ride a horse or a camel? if you travel to Oman, you don't have to choose - you cna do both!

  

Desert or Ocean?

Located in the Middle East, Oman is home to the legendary Empty Quarter, the largest sand desert in the world, which it shares with Saudi Arabia. Here, visitors can ride camels across towering dumes. watch the sunset paint the sand gold and gaze at the starts in a clear sky. But it is important that peopke avoid the summer months when temperatures can reach over 50 degree Celsius. In contract, the coast offers a cool escape. By day, visitors snorkeling among the colorful corals may spot turtles swimming through the water. From June to August, the sea turtles can be seen nesting on the beaches.

  

Modern or Ancint?

Any of Oman's legendary forts provide a perfect setting to learn more about Oman's history. Only two hours from the capital, Muscat, Nizwa Fort has been standing since the 1600s. Those preferring modern architecture can admire the mordern buildings that dot the capital city.

  

Luxury Malls or Local Markets?

Exporting oil has brought Oman considerable prosperity, and today's shoppers - both local and foreign - enjoy a wide range of goods. At the Mall of Oman, anyone can shop, dine and be entertained while staying cool indoors. For a more traditional experience, they can wander the lively souks, historical marketplaces. These timeless markets offer everything from spices to silver jewelry and frankincense, one of Oman's best-known product.

  

Mountain Climbs or Pool Plunges?

Should you be looking for magnificent views, check out the AI Hajar mountain range, which is less than 100 kilometers from the city of Nizwa. Most travelers go there to hike the Jebel Shams Balcony Walk or catch a glimpse of the sunset. In contrast to the rocky landscapes, wadi pools scattered across the country provide opportunities for swimming and water play. Formed in dry walleys and riverbeds, many of the pools come to life during the rainy season. Others, fed by natueal springs, are permanent.

  

Oman's Vision 2040 plan is working to grow the country in a smart and sustainable way. By visiting now, travelers can enjoy its natural beauty and warm culture while seeing a nation building for the future.

  

  

## Note

Oman's national dish, shuwa, features large pieces of lamb or goat that are soaked, wrapped in leaves and them cooked slowly for one to two days with hot coals in and underground sand oven. For special festivals, the whole animal is cooked.

  

Wadi Shab is perhaps Oman's most famous wadi. The area features a variety of pools and a waterfall inside a cave!
