# Air Pollution:  An Invisible Threat
From cooking fires to fossil fuels, air pollution is all around us

A mother leans over a wood-burning stove as her baby plays nearby in a cloud of smoke. A family of three on a scooter rides down a busy city street. A group of students walks past a chemical manufacturing plant on their way to school. These everyday scenes hint at a dark truth: Nearly everyone on Earth - 99 percent of the global population - breathes air that is more polluted than the World Health Organization (WHO)  guidelines allow. Air pollution causes around 8 million deaths each year, making it one of the leading risks to human health around the world. And people from low - and middle-income countries (LMICs) face the highest exposure, suffering almost eight times the mortality rates of high-income countries.

Vehicles, industrial production and burning fossil fuels cause outdoor air pollution, while cooking or heating with wood, dung or lamp oil over open fires or simple stoves is the primary cause of household air pollution. Tiny particles are released into the air by these two main types of air pollution. Once breathed into the lungs, these particles enter the blood stream and can damage the heart, lungs and other organs, leading to strokes, lung cancer, heart disease and chronic respiratory illnesses.


### Note
Air pollution kills millions each year, harming children the most. From household stoves to busy streets, the danger is everywhere - yet solutions exits. Clean air is essential and can change lives for the better.

Looking for a clean-air vacation? Finland, Estonia, Australia, New Zealand, Grenada, Iceland and Mauritius boasted the best air quality in 2023 according to WHO.
