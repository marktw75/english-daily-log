# Locker Secrets
Who is breaking into lockers?

It started with a photo. Someone had posted it on Owl-City Confessions - a shot of a wadded-up love letter sitting inside a locker next to a chipped pink pencil case. Ivy recognized the case as belonging to Sasha Moore. Sasha wasn't just popular. She was powerful and more than a little mean. 

By lunchtime, two more students had reported strange locker incidents. Nothing stolen - but mirrors were titled, notebooks opened to private pages, lipstick rubbed across the doors.

"This isn't a joke," Ivy said. "It's a message."

Dean nodded. "But how's someone getting in? These are combination locks. Even if you tried, you couldn't just guess the codes."

They walked down the hall after school, passing each of the lockers that had been hit. No scratches. No damage. All still locked. Ivy paused at the last one - Sasha's. It was decorated with shiny tape and a burst gold balloon still dangling from the handle. She started at it for a minute. "Dean, what's Sasha's birthday?" she asked.

He didn't even have to think, "November 7th. I wish I didn't know that, but she makes such a big deal about it online that it's hare not to." As he was speaking, Ivy reached for the dial. 11-0-7. Click. The lock popped open.

Dean blinked. "You've gotta to kidding me."

Ivy gave a small, grim smile. "I think we're onto something."

That night, Ivy sat, looking through her notebook. "All the victims are part of Sasha's friend group, "" she said on a call with Dean.

"Which means this isn't random," Dean replied. "It's targeted."

"Right," Ivy said, "So, who has the guts to target them?"

"Hold on. Found something, A comment thread from last year. Some anonymous user calling Sasha out for bullying. It said she picked on a girl named Mina."

"Mina Park?" Ivy said thoughtfully. "Our classmate Lila's older sister ..."

The next day, Ivy hung back near the lockers. Ten minutes later, she spotted Lila Park -  quiet, small and usually invisible - lingering by Sasha'a locker.

"Looking for something?" Ivy asked. Lila froze.

"I saw the photo," Ivy said. "And the locker combos? Sasha's was her birthday."

Lila hesitated, "Those girls are so full of themselves. Of course they use their birthdays. I didn't need to break in - I just checked their profiles."

Dean stepped beside Ivy. "Smart. But posting their secrets online? Not so smart."

Lila's voice trembled, "They hurt my sister. Made her cry in the bathroom every day for a week. I just wanted them to feel what that's like."

Ivy looked at her, "I get it. But you don't fix cruelty by copying it. Far from it. You just start a new cycle."

Lila looked down, "So what now?"

Ivy shrugged. "Now? You help us find a better way."
