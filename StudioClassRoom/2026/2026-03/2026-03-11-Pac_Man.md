# PAC-MAN 
The most successful arcade game of all time

Who knew that inspiration could come from pizza?  For Toru Iwatani that is exactly what happened when he grabbed a piece of pizza from a pizza box. The pizza left in the box looked like a mouth. The shape inspired him to create a character. Iwatani imagined a hungry man going through tunnels trying to eat as much food a fast as he could. Thus, Pac-Man was born. Pac-Man doesn't just "eat." He also is trying to dodge or eat Blinky, Pinky, Inky or Clyde, the colorful ghosts that are trying to catch him.

In the last 70s, video games were relatively new, and the most successful ones involved shooting things. Arcades were filled with teenage boys batting aliens in Space  invaders and other shooting games. Iwatani wanted to create a game that was not about violence and change the game culture. He mentioned that he intended to create a game that everyone including women in particular, could enjoy. Pac-Man fit the bill and still does. It continues to draw players of all ages to enjoy "eating".

### Note
The relationship between Pac-Man and the ghosts is one where they go against each other, but they don't hate each other.

Originally, Pac-Man was named PuckMan in Japan. The Japanese phrase "paku paku taberu" means to eat something up while producing a popping sound.

Pack-Man drew not only women but different generations to arcades to play.
### Key Point
Pac-Man is a popular video game created for everyone to enjoy.
