# Embracing Cultural Diversity
Learning about other cultures can make your lif better

You may never have thought about it, but much of your personality and many of your interests and values come from your culture. Your culture determines the language you speak, your tastes in food and music, your manners and your priorities. But exposure to different cultures can improve your life, inspire creativity and even help you understand your own culture better.

Had it not been for cultural diversity, a number of inventions would never have existed. For example, many believe that in the 1970s a Japanese immigrant in Canada named Hidekazu Joji wanted to sell sushi. But many locals were reluctant to try food with raw fish or seaweed. So, to cater to Canadian tastes, he developed a new sushi roll that used cooked crab with seaweed on the inside rather than the outside. His creation, which became known as the California roll, became very popular around the world and even in Japan.

Culture encompasses more than food and art, however. Cultures often hold different expectations for polite behavior. Sometimes this leads to travelers behaving rudely without meaning to, simply due to not knowing local customs.

Cultures also value different things, which can lead to disagreements and even tension. Is personal expression more important than performing your role in society? Is it more important to be on time or to stop to greet your neighbors? Disagreements on these points are often rooted in different cultural values.

And yet, challenging your own culture's ideas can help you grow. Sometimes, you may learn from others and change your views. Other times, you may appreciate some parts of your culture more now that you know they are not found everywhere.

Embracing cultural diversity requires courage as we have to take the risk of interacting with others who are not like us. It requires humility as we must recognize that our customs are not always right. It also requires patience and kindness to work through conflicts. But it leaves us with a richer understanding of the world and may even inspire us to create something new, like  California rolls or jazz.

### How to improve communication across cultures.
1. Become a good communicator in your own language
2. Be curious about people who are different from you
3. Learn to speak other languages
4. Don't hint at things, say what you want or need clearly
