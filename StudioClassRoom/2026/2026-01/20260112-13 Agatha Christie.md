# Agatha Christie

The Queen of Mystery is the bestselling novelist of all time

Agatha Christie's works

Christie wrote 66 detective novels and more than a dozen short-story collection. Her works have sold over 2 billion copies and have been translated into more than 100 languages.

  

Mystery. The word excites our curiosity. Mystery novels sell thousands of copies each year, and many readers count Dame Agatha Christie among their favorite authors.

  

Agatha Mary Clarissa Miller was born September 15, 1890, in Torquay, England. As she was growing up in the quiet town, her mother often told her stories. Agatha loved these stories that transported her into a world of fantasy. Her mother also encouraged her to write from a young age.

  

At 16, Christie went to a school in Paris to study piano and singing. She spend hours practicing and learned to play the piano very well, but she never made music a career because of stage fright.

  

At 22, she fell in love with Archie Christie. They got married in 1914 at the beginning of World War I. Archie fought as a soldier, and Christie worked as a nurse and later in a hospital drugstore. This experience gave her knowledge of poisons, which she later used in many of her mystery plots. In 1920, her first novel, The Mysterious Affair at Styles introduced the brilliant Belgian detective Hercule Poirot. Though the book was rejected by several publishers, it was eventually published later the same year.

  

After her first book, her career took off, and readers couldn't get enough of her characters, particularly Poirot. Over 30 of her novels feature him.

  

One of the most interesting events in Christie's life occurred in 1926, when she disappeared. Her car was found abandoned, and the public feared she had been kidnapped or harmed. It was not until 11 days later that she was discovered staying at a hotel under a false name. To this day, no one knows exactly why she vanished. Some believe it was due to stress after her mother's death and the breakup of her marriage. The Christie's divorced in 1928.

  

Christie also traveled widely, and while on a trip, she met her second husband, Max Mallowan, an archaeologist. They married in 1930 and often visited the Middle East. Christie used her travels as an inspiration for several books, including Murder on the Orient Express and Death on the Nile.

  

Agatha Christie was made a dame of the British Empire in 1971 for her contributions to literature. She passed away on January 12, 1976, at age 85. Her clever plots, unexpected twists and unforgettable characters continue to attract fans to her timeless works of mystery.
