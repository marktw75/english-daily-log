# An Unforgettable Northern Adventure

Where polar bears wander under dancing skies

Polar Bear Jail

Chruchill has a polar bear Jail! Polar bears live on the ice to hunt seals. When the ice melts, some of them wander into town and are put in jail until the bay freezes. Then they are released back on the ice far from town.


Join YouTuber and travel writer Ashley Castle Pittman on her polar-bear expedition.

  

If a once-in-a-lifetime adventure in the Arctic is on your bucket list, look no further than Churchill, Manitoba! This tiny, isolated, northern Canadian town, with a population of almost 900, is situated on Hudson Bay's windy shores. It can only be reached by plane, a 48-hour train ride of, in the dead of winter, an ice road. But a trip to "the polar bear capital of the world" is definitely worth it.

  

Beginning in late summer, one of nature's most spectacular events occurs here. Hundreds of polar bears make their way to this area to wait for Hudson Bay to freeze so they can hunt for seals, their main food source. In fact, more polar bears are in Churchill during the fall than people.

  

Visitors and scientists alike flock to Churchill to observe these incredible mammals close-up. You, too, can join them aboard a tundra buggy, a massive window-lined vehicle with huge tires. As it rolls across the icy landscape, you can safely get close to the bears without disrupting them. There is nothing like spotting a polar bear and her cubs navigating through the snow. Encountering a male on his way to catch his prey is also possibility.

  

While you're there, watch for the northern lights, also known as the aurora borealis! During winter especially, they spin around and dance in the night skies.

  

Dogs have always been a crucial part of Churchill's history, so take the opportunity to learn about them. Then befriend some sled dogs, and go on an adventure-filled dogsled ride through the forest.

  

Churchill offers and entirely different experience when summer returns as several thousand beluga whales migrate into the Chruchill River. They give birth and feed their babies while there. Join a kayak tour and float alongside the gentle creatures as they pop out of the water to watch you. Feeling brave? Try snorkeling among these friendly white whales!

  

Surprisingly, Churchill is a birdwatcher's paradise. More than 250 species of birds either build nests in, or pass through, Churchill and the surrounding area during their annual migrations.

  

Several historic locations also hold great interest. Learn about the French and English rivalry over control of the fur trade in the 1700s at the Prince of Wales Fort. And nearby Cape Merry houses equipment that helped defend the area during the same era.

  

Churchill isn't just a destination - it's an adventure like no other.
