# The Case of the Vanishing Violin

Who would want to make a violin disappear?

  

  

The first time Ivy Chen laid eyes on Jonah Park, he was seated on the school steps playing the violin, totally engrossed in the music. She sat down to listen, complimenting him afterward. By Monday, when he ran into Ivy again, his violin was gone.

  

"I left it in the practice room," he said, eyes wide with disbelief. "Locked the door and everything."

  

That same day, rumors started flying. Some said Jonah had faked the theft to get attention. Others whispered that it was an act of revenge carried out by a former friend consumed with jealousy.

  

Ivy didn't put much stock in rumors. And Jonah didn't fit the profile of a liar. After visiting the scene of the crime herself, she noticed that the door was secured with a digital keypad. Just then, Dean, her best friend and occasional hacker, came down the hallway and peered over her shoulder.

  

"Over 10,000 combinations." he said. "You'll never guess it."

  

"I wasn't going to guess. I was going to investigate," She said with a half-smile.

  

Dean gestured toward the keypad. "The code's for staff only. No one gets in unless a teacher punches it in for them ... unless someone was peeking."

  

Next stop was the office of the music teacher, Mr. Rolston.

  

"I gove the code to some students for a weekend club meeting," he began, a little flustered. "But I - uh - guess I forgot to reset it afterward," he said, adjusting his glasses. "Whoever wanted to could've - well, theoretically - gotten in."

  

Back in the library, Dean pulled out his laptop and opened the school's public Wi-Fi portal. "If the system were to log keypad activity, I would have a chance to retrieve a timestamp."

  

A minute later, he whistled. "Got it. Someone used the keypad at 5:17 p.m. on Friday."

  

Returning to the music wing, they scanned the bulletin board. A faded practice schedule caught Ivy's eye.

  

"Look," she said. "Friday at 5 ... Rolston's mentoring students then."

"And only a few students would've been here at that time," Ivy added. "Let's figure out who."

  

Cross-referencing the schedule with past signups, they found a name that stood out. Peter Lang, one of the cello players. He had been at every mentoring session - and he had a rocky history with Jonah.

  

Sure enough, at the next session, a familiar face slipped into the music room at 5:16 p.m. - Peter Lang.

  

After school, they confronted him.

  

"I didn't steal it," Peter burst out. "I just - Jonah acted like he was better than the rest of us. I hid it behind the ceiling tiles in practice room three. I was going to return it!"

  

Ivy crossed her arms, "But you didn't."

Peter looked down. "I just wanted to see him freak out a little."

  

Jonah got his violin back, and Peter was suspended for a week.

  

Later, Ivy found Peter. "I'm not excusing what you did," she said, "but you feel invisible, don't you?"

  

"Kind of," Peter sighed.

  

Ivy nodded. "Still, that wasn't very nice."

  

Peter didn't reply. But he began showing up early for practice and playing better.
