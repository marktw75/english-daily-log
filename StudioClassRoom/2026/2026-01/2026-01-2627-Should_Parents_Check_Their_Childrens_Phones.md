# Should Parents Check Their Children's Phones

Is privacy or safety more important?

  

Nowadays teens basically share their lives online. Most people know that the internet is full of dangers - cyberbullies, online predators and scams. Parents want to protect their kids from harm, even their teenagers. Some parents think monitoring their kid's texts and social media is the best way to do that.

  

But many kids don't want their parents to check up on them. They feel it's an invasion of privacy. Just because they're young doesn't mean they don't deserve trust.If parents read every message, they could push their teens away, causing the to hide things and create secret accounts.

  

Instead of what teens consider spying parents should have open conversations with their teens about staying safe online. They can set boundaries but also give their child the opportunity to show they're responsible. Feeling respected is important, and if something serious happens, their teen will be more likely to goto a parent for help.

  

What matters is that teens feel supported and safe, while parents feel confident their children are making smart choices online. Both trust and protection are important.

  

  

Emma: Ugh, my mom asked to see my phone last night so she could look at my texts. Can you believe that?

Liam: Wow! Honestly, though, I kind of get it, Parents just want to keep us safe.

  

Emma: But it's my phone. I'm not hiding anything, so it feels like she doesn't trust me.

Liam: I get that. But think about all the ugly stuff online - cyberbullying, scams, creepy people. Parents worry because they have heard about kids getting mixed up in things they don't know how to handle.

  

Emma: Sure, but going through my priavte messages won't make me safer. I don't like being spied on. I'm just going to create a secret account.

Liam: Hmm ... but what if someone was harassing you and you were too frightened to tell your parents? They'd see that and could step in and help.

  

Emma: Or my mom could just talk to me and trust me to come to her. I think parents should teach us howto handle things, not invade our privacy.

Liam: Yeah, maybe. I guess it's about balance - guidance without crossing the line.

  

Emma: Exactly. Trust goes both ways.
