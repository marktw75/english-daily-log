# Social Deduction Games

Sometimes it's hard to tell who's who

  

If you enjoy reading people and thinking on your feet, social deduction games might be for you. In these games, players must use reasoning, observation and communication to figure out each other's hidden roles or goals. Players must ask questions, watch for clues and guess the truth. The games often include being dishonest, keeping secrets and working as a team. Many people play these games in groups, a parties or online.

  

In a social deduction game, some roles are good, and some are bad. The good players are trying to find the bad players. The bad players are trying to confuse other players and avoid being identified. During each round, players talk and make guesses. They try to determine who is telling the truth. The game ends when all bad guys are caught for the good team is defeated.

  

Some social deduction games are simple and rely on cards or paper. Others are online and use fun stories and characters. No matter which kind you play, the goal remains the same. To win, you must use your mind, listen carefully and work together.

## Key Point

Social deduction games are fun group activities where players use clues and teamwork to win.

### Terms to know

Role: Your character or job in the game

Imposter: Player that works against the group without them knowing

Alibi: Your excuse to avoid making people suspicious

Vote: Deciding who has to leave the game
