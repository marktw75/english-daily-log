# Star Wars Day

How a joke contributed to a phenomenon that spread around the world

by Heather Lin

## Article

Every May 4, fans around the globe celebrate *Star Wars*, the beloved movies set "in a galaxy far, far away." Why that date? It's because "May the Fourth" sounds like "May the Force," part of the films' famous line, "May the Force be with you." The pun "May the Fourth be with you" is a clever twist on that classic line.

The first *Star Wars* film made its debut in 1977 and did far better than was expected. However, the pun did not appear until 1979. And it had nothing to do with the films. It was used in a British newspaper on May 4 of that year to congratulate Margaret Thatcher on becoming prime minister.

Over time, *Star Wars* fans adopted the phrase and gave it a new meaning. Though the date has not been formally recognized as an official holiday, Star Wars Day has been welcomed by fans. They mark the date with movie marathons, parties and other events.

What began as a joke shows how language, culture and creativity can turn a movie line into a worldwide celebration.

## Interesting Facts about Star Wars

- *Star Wars* has inspired countless books, TV shows, games and events. Scientists even named a bug species after Jedi Master Yoda: *Trigonopterus yoda*.
- The first *Star Wars* movie opened on May 29, 1977. Over half the U.S. population saw it that summer, and it remains one of the most successful movies ever made.
- The phrase "I have a bad feeling about this" is spoken in every *Star Wars* film.

## Key Point

- Fans around the world celebrate May 4 as Star Wars Day, proving how a clever joke can connect people.

## Focus on Phrases

to do with 與……有關

## Usage Tip

### Tip 1
to do with

`to do with` 通常與 `have something to do with`、`have nothing to do with` 等詞連用，表示「與……有關」或「與……毫無關係」。其中 `do` 並不是「做」的字面意思，而是表示「涉及、關聯」。例如：`This problem has to do with money.` 這個問題和金錢有關。若是否定用法，`have nothing to do with` 則表示「與……毫無關係」，例如：`I have nothing to do with that rumor.` 我和那個謠言毫無關係。

### Tip 2
What began as ...

`What began as ...` 用來表示「原本是……的事物，後來……」，常見於描述事物、關係或情況的轉變。句首的 `What` 引導名詞子句，作主詞使用，意思為「起初是……」。例如：`What began as a small hobby turned into a successful business.` 起初只是個小興趣，後來變成一門成功的事業。這個結構常搭配 `turn into`、`develop into`、`become` 等，來表達變化的過程。再如：`What began as a friendly discussion turned into a heated debate.` 原本友善的討論變成了激烈的辯論。
