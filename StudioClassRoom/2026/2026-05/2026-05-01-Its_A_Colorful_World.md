# It's a Colorful World

How people named colors reveals history, culture and the way humans saw the world

by Linda Ludwig

## Article

Why is red called "red" or yellow called "yellow"? A glimpse into the history of early color use by ancient civilizations gives a clue. The origins of most English color words came from Proto-Indo-European (PIE) languages.

Linguistic studies have found that languages developed color words in a very specific order. Ancient languages only divided the world into dark and light, so black and white were the earliest color words, standing for night and day and basic contrasts.

The next color named was red. Often linked to power and energy, it was symbolic of strength and courage. In fact, red was the color of military uniforms in many empires throughout history.

Yellow, which came next, goes back to a PIE root meaning to shine brightly. Yellow represents brightness, life and energy.

After yellow came green, from a PIE root that means to grow. In nearly every language and culture, green is connected to new growth, plants and nature.

Historically, one of the last major colors named was blue. Before blue became a recognized color, people often used "sky" or "sea" to describe what is now classified as blue.

Whatever names we call them, colors make our lives richer.

## Key Point

- Ancient languages named colors in a specific historical order, revealing how nature, culture and early human experience shaped the words we use for colors today.

## Usage Tip

### Tip 1
Whatever + S + V, S + V

`whatever` 引導讓步副詞子句時，表示「不論……，……」。例如：Whatever you do, do it with all your heart. 無論你做什麼，都要全心去做。這裡的 `whatever` 也可以用 `no matter what` 取代：Whatever/No matter what challenges we face, we will overcome them together. 不論我們面臨什麼挑戰，都會一起克服。

此外，`whatever` 也可引導名詞子句，當主詞或受詞使用。例如：I'll support whatever choice you make. 無論你做什麼選擇，我都支持。
