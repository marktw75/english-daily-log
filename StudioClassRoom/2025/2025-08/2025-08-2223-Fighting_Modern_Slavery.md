# Fighting Modern Slavery

This great evil is not all in the past

by Elizabeth Koram

Tomorrow is the International Day for the Remembrance of the Slave Trade and its abolition, a day dedicated to remembering a dark period of the world's history and all the suffering that came from it. The abolition of the slave trade did make the world more just, but slavery is still pervasive despite being illegal. In fact, almost 50 million people are in slavery today, more than twice as many as were taken from Africa between 1400 and 1900. This huge number of people includes those in all variety of situations, but all are being exploited. Usually, victims are forced to do some kind of labor, but forced marriages are also considered modern slavery.

The most widespread form of slavery today is debt bondage. This occurs when a person living in poverty borrows money and is required to work to pay back the debt. Usually, the wages are so low that it takes far longer than it should to pay off the debt. Sometimes interest and living expenses are added to the debt so that it can never be paid back. The COVID-19 pandemic made this type of slavery even more common because many people did not have access to legitimate loans while facing new economic pressures.

When most people think of modern slavery, human trafficking comes to mind. Human trafficking involves moving people a significant distance to exploit them. Some victims are physically forced to go, while others are tricked into complying. Once people are moved, especially if they cross international borders, they find leaving their situation nearly impossible.

Addressing modern slavery requires cooperation among people and organizations. Although slavery is illegal in most countries, government policies can improve the enforcement of these laws and mitigate situations that put people at risk, such as poverty.

Businesses also have a role to play. Any product that consumers purchase has passed through numerous individuals and corporations, and most of these supply chains contain some companies that use forced labor. Thus, businesses need to not only avoid exploiting those themselves but also investigate the companies they buy from. This may result in the final product being more expensive, but preventing exploitation is worth it.

Individuals can contribute time or money to the many organizations that fight slavery. They can also learn to recognize signs that a stranger may be a trafficking victim and what actions they can take to help.

Ending slavery for good will require people and companies to join forces in a relentless effort to eliminate this practice.

## Key Points

- Despite the abolition of the slave trade, modern slavery, including debt bondage and forced labor, remains pervasive, affecting nearly 50 million people worldwide, especially those living in poverty.
- Ending modern slavery, particularly human trafficking and forced labor, requires cooperation among governments, businesses and individuals to enforce laws, prevent exploitation and support anti-slavery initiatives.

## Usage Tip
### Tip 1
`dedicated to + V-ing/N`

動詞 `dedicate` 為「貢獻、獻出（所有時間或精力）」之意。本字搭配介系詞 `to`，形成 `dedicate N to`（把某物奉獻給……）或以被動型態 `be dedicated to`（致力於……），由於 `to` 為介系詞，所以後方須接名詞 `N` 或動名詞 `V-ing`。例句：The activist dedicated his life to environmental conservation.

### Tip 2
`... not only ... but also ...`

`not only A but also B` 意指「不僅 A，還有 B」，說明 A 與 B 兩者兼具，但重點主要落在 B 上。由於該表達為一種對等連接的結構，所以 A 與 B 的詞性要一致，以確保句子的對稱，如本句中的 `avoid` 與 `investigate` 均為動詞。此外，該結構中的 `also` 可以省略，意思不變。例句：Ms. Smith is not only a teacher but (also) a great mentor.
