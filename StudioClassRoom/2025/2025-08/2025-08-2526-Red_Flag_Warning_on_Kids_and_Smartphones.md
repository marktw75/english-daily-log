# Red Flag Warning on Kids and Smartphones Long Overdue

The power of smartphones

by Lori Borgman

In 2024 we were given a clear warning for cellphones. It came in the form of a book titled The Anxious Generation: How the Great Rewiring of Childhood Is Causing an Epidemic of Mental Illness by Jonathan Haidt.

When I read the book, I underlined paragraph after paragraph and tagged page after page with notes. I became hooked on underlining and tagging in a book about becoming hooked.

Haidt thoroughly connects the dots between the rise in mental illness among teens and the rise in popularity of social media on smartphones. He makes a case that social media is not appropriate for children.

Social media platforms are designed to be addictive. Haidt points out that the problem is not just what social media does, but what social media keeps people from doing. Today's teens are less likely to go out with friends. They are less likely to get a driver's license, less likely to play sports and more likely to be alone on their phones. Some teens sleep with their phones, texting throughout the night.

It starts early. Who hasn't observed young children in airports and grocery carts fascinated by moving screens? The brain patterning for constantly changing images is being imprinted.

I saw a clip of a basketball game featuring a contest. Mothers lined up and their babies were lined up about 10 yards away. It was a race to see which babies would get to their mothers first.

How did the mothers entice them? By waving their cellphones. What sets Haidt apart from screaming "Fire!" is that he identifies ways to put out the flames:

- Say no to smartphones before high school. [Feature phones] are fine in middle school for safety and staying connected.
- Say no to social media before age 16. Australia just passed a law to that effect. Once kids are capable of mature decision making, they are less likely to become addicted.
- Finally, he points a finger at adults. Adults are powerful influencers when it comes to cellphones.

Haidt is the red flag on the beach, signaling the presence of dangerous and life-threatening tides.

## Key Points

- The Anxious Generation warns that social media, which is designed to be addictive, reduces teens' real-world social interactions and activities.
- The author urges people to put limits on the use of smartphones for children and recommends that adults model healthy behavior when it comes to phones.

## Usage Tip
### Tip 1
`less likely`

形容詞 `likely` 表「有可能的」，後方加副詞不定詞 `to V`，表示「某事發生機率」。前方若加上比較級 `less`（較少地），會使 `likely` 的可能性降低，表「較不可能（做某事）」。但並非不等於 `unlikely`（不太可能的），若是要表達某時候住處會停電，即可將 `less` 改為 `more`（較多地）。例句：Without clear instructions, people are more likely to make mistakes.

### Tip 2
`be + being + p.p.`

此為英文的進行被動，進行式與被動語態的結合，意指主角正位處於「被……」的動作之中。進行被動須用 `being + 過去分詞 p.p.`，且前面的 be 動詞有 `is/am/are`（現在式）、`was/were`（過去式）或 `will be`（未來式／假設語氣）呈現。例句：The road was being repaired when the accident happened.
