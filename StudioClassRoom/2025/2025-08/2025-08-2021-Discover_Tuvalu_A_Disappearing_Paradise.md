# Discover Tuvalu: A Disappearing Paradise

Experience its beauty and join the fight against rising seas

by Christine Bedwell

In the South Pacific lies a peaceful paradise that may soon vanish. Although aware of the situation, the people of Tuvalu, a breathtaking island nation between Australia and Hawaii, welcome you to their remote and precious home.

Begin your journey on Funafuti, Tuvalu's capital and largest island. Browse for souvenirs, enjoy the seafood and ask about local events. At festivals, you can experience Tuvalu's close community, traditional dances, food and a long Tuvalu's national ball game that ends with silly performances. Or befriend locals during an afternoon game of soccer or volleyball at the airport. The runway, when not in use, is perfect for sports. Looking for adventure? Go snorkeling among the coral reefs to spy on the diverse marine life, or go diving to explore shipwrecks. The lagoons, channels and ocean waters within the Funafuti Marine Conservation Area hold treasures that make for remarkable underwater experiences.

Prefer to stay above the water? Visit the nesting grounds of the endangered green sea turtle, which is also in the Funafuti Marine Conservation Area. Or island-hop on a boat across Tuvalu's nine main islands.

With its tranquil beauty, warm hospitality and rich traditions, Tuvalu offers an unforgettable escape unlike any other.

One of the world's smallest and least-visited nations, Tuvalu has gained global attention for its fight against rising seas. With its 26 square kilometers of land lying just 2 meters above sea level, Tuvalu's existence is under threat. America's National Aeronautics and Space Administration (NASA) scientists predict that by 2050, tides will submerge half of Funafuti, where most Tuvaluans live.

Despite contributing little to climate change, Tuvalu suffers its worst effects. Sea levels there are rising one and a half times faster than the global average, and worsening storms are destroying crops and contaminating fresh water. Many Tuvaluans have left. Over 4,000 already live in New Zealand. And as of 2025, a 2023 treaty between Tuvalu and Australia will allow 280 Tuvaluans per year to move there.

To buy time, Tuvalu is building sea walls and artificial land. It has introduced salt-resistant crops and above-ground gardening. Preparing for the worst, the country has changed its constitution to preserve its ocean borders and country status even if the land disappears. Twenty-five nations officially support this, 18 of which have made similar changes to their constitutions.

Tuvalu's spirit remains strong despite the challenges. With global support and innovation, the island paradise can continue to exist.

## Key Points

- The remote island nation of Tuvalu offers rich traditions, warm hospitality and marine adventures but is in danger of disappearing.
- Tuvalu faces rising seas but fights to survive with sea walls, artificial land, relocation options and legal protections while seeking global support.

## Usage Tip
### Tip 1
`Appositive`

句中的 `a breathtaking island nation` 是 `Tuvalu` 的同位語，亦即與 `Tuvalu` 是相同事物。同位語為名詞結構，用以補充說明前方名詞，尤其當前方名詞為專有名詞或陌生概念時，會特別需要同位語較白話的補述。此外，若同位語為非必要的補充資訊，應以逗號與前方名詞隔開。例句：Tesla, an electric car manufacturer, is known for its innovative technology.

### Tip 2
`S1 + be/V + three times/four times/etc. + more (+N)/adj.-er/adv.-er + than + S2`

本句使用「倍數 + 比較級 + than」的結構，表示 `S1` 與 `S2` 的倍數差異，說明「`S1` 比起 `S2` 在某狀態上多了幾倍」。其中，比較級的部分，能以名詞（`more + N`）、形容詞或副詞等形式出現，如本句中的 `faster` 即為副詞，用以修飾動詞 `are rising`。例句：In this country, city dwellers have three times more access to healthcare than rural residents.
