# Too Good to Be True

How virtual celebrities are changing entertainment

by David Morton

She sings, dances and interacts with her sea of adoring fans as her electric blue hair glows under the stage lights. But there's something different about this pop star - she doesn't physically exist. The digital superstar dancing on stage is Hatsune Miku, a virtual celebrity, and she's part of a growing trend that transcends the boundary between the physical and digital worlds.

Virtual celebrities, also known as (AKA) virtual influencers, are digital characters that are created using a blend of technologies including computer-generated images (CGI) and artificial intelligence (AI). They are designed to look and behave like real people and are able to sing, dance, model clothing, endorse products, engage with fans on social media and even advocate for causes. While some people embrace this new trend, others reject it for fear that interactions with digital personalities might lack credibility.

What makes 16-year-old Hatsune Miku special is that she is a collaboration between human creativity and technology and "sings" over 100,000 songs created by individual fans. Her voice is a product of a complex voice synthesizer called Vocaloid, and her performances are entirely digital. She has even performed with major artists like Lady Gaga and Pharrell Williams. Miku's live concerts, where holograms or LED screens are used to bring her to "life," have sold out in cities around the world.

One of the world's most beloved virtual influencers, Lu, came from humble beginnings. Lu, a digital being created by the Brazilian retail company Magazine Luiza, started as a virtual assistant for online shoppers. However, her popularity soared, and she quickly became the face of the brand. Lu has also worked with top brands like Adidas and McDonald's and starred in music videos with famous artists - even appearing on Brazil's version of Dancing with the Stars. She is a great example of how a digital celebrity can bridge the gap between entertainment, social issues and marketing. And with her support of women's rights, Lu continues to break new ground for virtual influencers.

### Lil Miquela

Miquela Sousa, better known as Lil Miquela, is a virtual influencer who has taken the pop music world by storm. Created by the tech company Brud, Miquela has appeared in major campaigns for brands like Prada and Calvin Klein, and her music has topped the charts. What sets Miquela apart is her advocacy for social causes such as immigration rights and racial equality.

Despite their success, virtual influencers aren't without controversy, with many expressing discomfort with the idea of "fake humans" interacting with the world as if they were real. For example, Meta's experiment with AI-powered profiles on Facebook and Instagram faced criticism when users expressed disgust at strange chat interactions, culturally insensitive content and very strange pictures found on their profiles. This led to viral screenshots being shared online.

As we look ahead, it's worth asking: How will the world adjust to this new reality where digital beings exist alongside real humans? The answer, as always, lies in how we choose to use the tools we have been given.

## Key Points

- A collaboration between human creativity and technology, virtual celebrities like Hatsune Miku transcend the separation between the physical and digital world and can dance, sing and even endorse products for companies.
- Although the popularity of some virtual influencers has soared over recent years, many people still feel discomfort with the idea of sharing the social media world with AI-powered profiles on platforms like Facebook.

## Usage Tip
### Tip 1
`S + V + for fear that + S + would/might + VR`

本結構 `for fear that` 意為「就怕說......，以免......」，用來說明由於有對某事情的擔憂，所以選擇做某事。令人擔憂之事以 `that` 子句帶出，子句中通常使用助動詞 `would` 或 `might` 來表達該事發生的「可能性」。此外，擔憂之事也能以 `of + N/V-ing` 的形式呈現。例句：We lowered our voices for fear of being overheard (= for fear that we would/might be overheard).

### Tip 2
`... not without controversy`

該表達中的 `not without` 意為「並非沒有」，也就是「有」的意思。此為雙重否定的結構，利用否定來加強肯定的語氣。該結構可用於推翻或調整讀者的預期，如本句前面提及 virtual influencers 的成功，可能令人誤以為 virtual influencers 毫無缺點，此處透過此表達來強調出 virtual influencers 也存在爭議。例句：The widely used treatment is not without risks as it may lead to such side effects as nausea.
