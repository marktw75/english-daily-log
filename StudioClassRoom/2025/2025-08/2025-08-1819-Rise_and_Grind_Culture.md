# "Rise and Grind" Culture

"Strive not to be a success, but rather to be of value." - Albert Einstein

by Pamela Osment

Do you hit the ground running as soon as you open your eyes in the morning? Are you guilty of constantly complaining that there aren't enough hours in the day to accomplish your tasks? If so, you might be part of the "grind culture," also known as the "hustle culture." It emphasizes the belief that the more you work, the more successful you will be. The culture involves sacrificing your personal time to work toward your professional goals.

According to Campbell Walker, a previous member of the hustle culture, "Hustle culture equates your worth with your work. It encourages you to focus [only] on work, as opposed to everything else around you that makes your life rich."

This is a trend that plenty of people have adopted. After all, many of us live in societies that celebrate hard work and look down on those who appear lazy. However, some are beginning to back away from this trend as it is creating problems in their lives.

Social media has played a major role in motivating and fostering the grind culture. Young professionals are online, boasting about their rise and grind way of life. They're proud of the fact that they hustle seven days a week. Their videos promote the idea that rest is for the weak and that a person's value comes from their productivity.

Some people are discovering, though, how harmful this can be. Kianna, a successful social media influencer, appeared to have mastered the grind. But with just four hours of sleep each night, her life was falling apart, and she was experiencing burnout. Kianna began therapy and learned the critical lesson of work-life balance. She also learned that she needed to regularly make time for herself.

Communications expert and coach Brooks E. Scott says that attitudes about the grind culture are changing. "To get ahead, you also need some creative space to rest ... you can't [find it] if you're always busy."

We must take a healthy approach to becoming successful and learn to make well-being a priority without sacrificing achievement.

## Key Points

- The hustle culture believes that the more you work, the more successful you will be.
- Though the hustle culture is popular, some people say that it can cause health problems.

## Usage Tip
### Tip 1
`equate A with B`

動詞 `equate`（使對等，同等看待）衍生自形容詞 `equal`（同等的），常與介系詞 `with` 連用，形成 `equate A with B`，表「認為 A 和 B 相等」，本句即意為奮鬥文化將你的價值和工作畫上等號，會以你的工作來衡量你的價值。請留意，`equate` 表達的是一種主觀判斷，但 A 與 B 在客觀上未必真的相等。例句：We shouldn't equate real influence with social media popularity.

### Tip 2
`who/which + (N)/how + adj./adv./what/why/etc. clause`

本句的 `how harmful this can be` 為名詞子句，作動詞 `discovering` 的受詞，可經由 `how` 帶出一個要外加修飾的程度。類似的名詞子句也可用 `who`、`which`、`what`、`how`、`why` 等字詞來引導。`S + can be + V` 亦可出現在這類名詞子句中，表示「某件事是如何」。例句：Who wins the election will determine the country's future.
