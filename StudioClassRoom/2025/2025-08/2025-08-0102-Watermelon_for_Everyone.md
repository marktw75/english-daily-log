# Watermelon for Everyone!

Enjoy this season's favorite fruit

by Pamela Osment

Wouldn't a big piece of watermelon taste great right now? This delicious fruit is the perfect treat during the dog days of summer.

When I was growing up in the South, watermelons were plentiful. I thought, perhaps, our region was the birthplace of watermelons. But research shows that the Americas were probably one of the last places where this delightful fruit was grown.

Ancient Egyptians grew and ate watermelons. Yet the true origin remained a mystery until 2021. A research paper published that year suggested that today's modern watermelon are closely related to the wild melons of Sudan. This discovery caused some scientists to conclude that the fruit began its global journey in northeast Africa.

Documents show that the fruit found its way to China in the 10th century. Other records show Native Americans planting watermelons in the mid-1500s. They quickly found their way into many American gardens, including those of America's third president, Thomas Jefferson. By the 1800s, American cookbooks contained recipes for watermelon pickles, jellies and more. Today, the fruit is enjoyed in salads, fruit drinks and on the grill.

Besides tasting great, a slice of watermelon is good for you. The juicy fruit is rich in vitamins A and C, as well as a variety of other nutrients, and is about 92 percent water. Vitamin C strengthens your immune system, and vitamin A is good for skin and eye health. The fruit is believed to protect the body from cancer and is good for heart health. Watermelon juice is thought to relieve muscle tension, too. This refreshing fruit is sweet, but low in calories. Did you know that it is considered a vegetable as well as a fruit? The U.S. government does because it is grown in a field and harvested like other vegetables.

Melons come in lots of different sizes. So far, the largest watermelon on record weighed nearly 159 kilograms! As for shapes, most melons are round or oval. However, Japan has perfected the art of growing square watermelons!

If you're searching for a healthy snack this summer, look no further than a piece of healthy, delicious watermelon. It's truly a summer delight!

## Tips for picking a good watermelon

- Pick a heavy one. That means it's juicy and sweet.
- Choose round melons; they are usually sweeter than oval melons.
- Knock on it. If it has a deep sound, the fruit has more water and is probably ready to eat.
- Find the "field spot" - the spot on the fruit's surface that shows where the watermelon rested on the ground. If it is creamy yellow or green-orange, the melon is full of flavor. If the spot is white or light yellow, the watermelon lacks flavor.

## Key Points

- A delicious piece of watermelon is the perfect summer treat.
- Watermelon provides plenty of health benefits.
