# The Rise of Amanda Gorman

A symbol of hope

by Christine Bedwell

Poet, activist and model, Amanda Gorman is no stranger to struggle. She describes herself as a skinny Black girl descended from slaves and raised by a single mother. She listed race, gender and family background as challenges she faced. In addition, she had a speech problem that made communication difficult, yet Gorman has become a distinguished poet.

Gorman was born too early, resulting in her speech problem. In fact, it wasn't until around age 20 that she overcame her struggle to pronounce R's thanks to therapy. Gorman sometimes felt underestimated because of her speech problem. But encouraged by her mother, a public-school English teacher, she found her voice in poetry. Gorman sees her speech problem as a strength. It caused her to notice sound, pitch and pronunciation and taught her to create powerful poetry by carefully selecting her words.

Gorman's poetry addresses issues such as oppression, equality and the climate crisis, inspiring people to change. At 16 she became a youth delegate to the United Nations and Los Angeles' first Youth Poet Laureate. By 19, while studying on scholarship at Harvard, she toured the U.S. as the first National Youth Poet Laureate. She graduated with honors in 2020.

On January 20, 2021, 22-year-old Gorman recited her poem The Hill We Climb at U.S. President Joe Biden's inauguration. She made history as the youngest inaugural poet in U.S. history. Her message of overcoming obstacles through unity and hope resonated deeply, earning her widespread recognition.

Her appearance has opened doors to modeling contracts, interviews and talk-show appearances. Gorman's new challenge is deciding which opportunities to accept. Her mother's recommendation to always be true to herself reminds her to choose projects that align with her values and ambitions.

Gorman's picture has appeared on the covers of Glamour, Variety, Porter and Vogue magazines, accompanied by interviews in which she discusses issues that are near to her heart. She has published two children's books and likewise released poetry collections. In the future, she hopes to be a novelist, and as soon as she turns 35, she wants to become the president of the U.S.

The closing lines of The Hill We Climb read, "There is always light. If only we're brave enough to see it. If only we're brave enough to be it." Her journey shows how, when one faces obstacles and has the courage to see them as opportunities, incredible things can happen.

## Key Points

- Amanda Gorman overcame the obstacles of race, gender and speech problems to find her voice and to become a distinguished poet who inspires change using poetry.
- As the youngest inaugural poet in the U.S., Gorman has led to publishing several books and making history through work that aligns with her values, following her mother's recommendation to focus on what matters.

## Usage Tip
### Tip 1
`result in, result from, the result of`

這三個與 `result` 有關的片語均用來表達「因果關係」。片語動詞 `result in` 表「造成」，前接因，後接果；`result from` 意為「起於……」，前接果，後接因；`A be the result of B` 表「A 是 B 的結果」，所以 A 為果，B 為因，該用法中的 `result` 則為名詞。例句：The accident was the result of (= resulted from) careless driving.

### Tip 2
`If only` vs. `only if`

慣用語 `if only` 意為「只要；倘若」；若表示強烈希望或遺憾的「但願，要是……就好了」，則搭配假設語氣。使用假設語氣時，若期望之事與現在事實相反，動詞用過去式；與過去事實相反，動詞則用過去完成式 `had + p.p.`。`Only if` 則用來限定唯一條件，表「只有在（某條件下）才能……」。例句：Children can watch this film only if they are accompanied by parents.
