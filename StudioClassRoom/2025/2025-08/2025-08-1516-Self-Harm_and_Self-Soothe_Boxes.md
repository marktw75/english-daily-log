# Self-Harm and Self-Soothe Boxes

Replacing dangerous responses to distress with healthy responses

by Elizabeth Karamouzis

Self-harm is an unhealthy coping mechanism for emotional pain. Boxes of items that help ease a person's pain provide safer alternatives by engaging the senses, offering distraction and relief while individuals seek long-term help for underlying mental health challenges.

Many people around the world struggle to cope with severe mental health issues. One surprising but relatively common coping mechanism is self-harm. Most people try to avoid pain, but some seek out ways to inflict pain on themselves because the physical pain distracts them from their emotional pain. In other cases, mental health problems, such as depression, can make people feel numb, and feeling pain seems better than feeling nothing.

Self-harm, however, is not a healthy coping mechanism because people who use it run the risk of hurting themselves more seriously than they intended. Self-harm also provides only short-lived relief; it can produce feelings of shame that increase isolation, and it can also be difficult to stop.

People who are tempted by self-harm eventually need help with the trauma or mental illness that created the urge in the first place. But in the short term, they need other ways of dealing with their distress. For people trying to resist the urge to harm themselves, other sources of stimulation may help. They can provide some of the distraction and relief that people get from self-harm without the risks. Some experts recommend collecting these alternatives in what is called a self-soothe box. That way, in moments of distress, the person can simply open the box.

Some companies sell self-soothe boxes online, but some users prefer to assemble the items in the boxes themselves. This lets them choose substitutes for self-harm that appeal to them personally. Some experts recommend that self-soothe boxes should include objects that engage a variety of senses. Objects to look at could include photographs of loved ones or happy memories, cards with encouraging quotes or pretty objects such as jars of glitter. For hearing, headphones and links to calming or uplifting music can be included. Things to smell, such as essential oils, may also help. And tactile objects such as fidget toys or stress balls can be especially helpful as replacements for self-harm.

If the urge to engage in self-harm is especially strong, a closer substitute may be necessary. Some people draw on their arms with red pen when they feel inclined to cut themselves, which can relieve some of their distress without doing damage. People can also wear spiky bracelets to roll along their arms or to rub, which causes some pain but without harm.

Self-soothe boxes will not resolve the problems that move people toward self-harm in the first place, but they can provide some relief in difficult moments. Helping people stay safe in moments of crisis is also important, buying them time to find more lasting help.

### The Senses and Mental Health

During mental-health crises, people tend to become so focused on their thoughts that they are not very aware of the world around them. Thus, one common exercise for people experiencing panic attacks involves identifying what they are experiencing using each of the five senses. Self-soothe boxes follow a similar principle.

## Key Points

- Self-harm is an unhealthy coping mechanism that provides only short-lived relief, but safer alternatives, such as self-soothe boxes, can help individuals manage distress.
- Self-soothe boxes offer personal, safe substitutes to self-harm by engaging the senses, helping individuals cope with distress in healthier ways.

## Usage Tip
### Tip 1
`What is called ...`

該表達同 `the thing which is called ...`，意為「被稱作是……的事物，所謂的……」，後方接上該事物的名稱，藉以引導讀者理解一個較不熟悉的概念。由於讀者對該名稱不熟，若直接出現該名稱，少了 `what is called` 的引入，將會使表達略顯唐突。此外，若該名稱是複數，該表達中的 `is` 須改為 `are`。例句：Ants communicate through what are called pheromones, chemical substances that trigger social behavior.

### Tip 2
`S1 + recommend ... + (that) + S2 + (should) + VR`

動詞 `recommend`（建議）後方可接 `that` 子句表達建議之事，其中 `that` 可省略。為表現該建議有執行之必要，`that` 子句中會搭配助動詞 `should`，但直接省略 `should` 亦正確。請注意，若 `S2` 為單數，省略 `should` 後，後方仍是接原形動詞 `VR`。此外，同樣能表「建議、要求」的動詞（如 `suggest`、`demand` 等）亦適用此用法。例句：The customer demanded (that) the store (should) replace the damaged product.
