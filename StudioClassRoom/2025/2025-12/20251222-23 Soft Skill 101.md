# Soft Skill 101

How you work is as important as what you know

Confidence, Teamwork, Problem Solving, Communication, Time Management, Self Motivation, High EQ

  

So, you're ready for that job interview, and you're backed by the right credentials. But can you demonstrate that you have more to offer than just the hard skills you learned in school? Your hard skills, things that are technical and job specific, are only part of the package.

  

It's also important to bring soft skills to the job. These are personal qualities and social abilities that help you work well with others. You either have them innately or gain them through experience. Some examples of these skills are **communication**, **teamwork**, **time management**, **problem solving** and **critical thinking**.

  

Hard skill may open doors, but soft skills will keep you in the room. For example, the hard skill doctors need, such as an understanding of biology and medicine, make them acceptable doctors. But adding soft skills, like the ability to listen and communicate clearly. makes them great doctors.

  

Successful businesses require people with a technical knowledge of hard skills as well as person-based soft skills. So, any smart worker knows it's best to grow both their hard and soft skills as much as possible.

  

The need for soft skills is especially true in today's professional landscape. With some people working from home or remotely, communication has become more complex. Being able to speak with as well as understand and maintain relationships with coworkers through a computer screen is a challenge. But it is one that modern employers expect their workers to meet.

  

While some are blessed with good soft skills, maybe you have to work at them. There are different ways to improve yourself in this area. The more effort you put into learning soft skills, the more you'll benefit. To strengthen your ability to communicate, allow yourself to meet and work with many different kinds of people. To build your problem-solving skills, work on tasks that will test your brain, Also, never be afraid to seek out more formal training, such as conferences or online webinars.

  

During your job interview, boast about what you've learned in and out of school, Show the interviewer that you will always be a student - learning new things forever. That willingness to learn is an essential soft skill.

  

## Key Points

- Soft skills are as important as hard skills on the job.
- Tasks that test your brain will help strengthen your problem-solving skills.

### Fun Fact

Hard and soft are adjectives that can be used in many ways! You can even speak with a hard or soft voice (a stern or a gentle voice). A ruler can have hard or soft power. Hard power is visible (like an army), while soft power is invisible (like a good relationship with other countries)
