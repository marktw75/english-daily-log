# A Sunny Christmas in Singapore

Exchange your sleigh bells for skyline views and tropical cheer

  

Did you know?

Singapore's Orchard Road Christmas light-up has been a tradition since 1984.

Orchard Road's Christmas light-up stretches for more than three kilo meters, making it one of Asia's fanciest Christmas streets.

Christmas's is a public holiday because it is celebrated by people of all faiths as a cultural and commercial festival.

  

Who says Christmas has to be snowy or cold? In sunny Singapore, the holiday takes one a special charm with bright skies, a light breeze and a whole lot of cheer. By day, the city's start of the season, Orchard Road, draws curious tourists and excited locals. A colorful swirl of sights, smells and sounds gets everyone feeling merry and bright. You might mistake it for a summer festival if not for the holiday decorations. People stroll past street performances, snap photos and enjoy seasonal music.

  

By mid-December, Christmas celebrations are in full swing with visitors admiring installations that illuminate the roads and bring sparkle to nearby buildings and sparks.

  

A great spot to explore is the Christmas Village located near the Ngee Ann City shopping mall. Here, you'll find everything from traditional European threats to local suisine. At the same time, you'll be treated to panoramic views that help everyone get into the spirit of the season.

  

If you wish to enjoy snow this Christmas, visit Snow City, Singapore's first indoor snow center. This magical escape stays at minus 5 degrees Celsius, allowing you to experience a memorable time in a winter playground without leaving the island.

  

When the sun sets, Singapore changes into a wonderful spectacle. Head to Christmas Wonderland at Gardens by the Bay to find a merry mix of activities for every kind of visitor. A giant outdoor fair lights up the area with cheer. The fairground is filled with light displays, carnival rides and performances - all of which delight visitors into the night.

  

You can also take a walk beneath the beautiful light sculptures. And don't miss the stalls that sell treats and thoughtful gifts, each one perfect for sharing the joy of Christmas with a loved one.

  

Seeking a more sophisticated experience? The Supertree Observatory offers a quiet place to enjoy the glowing skyline. Standing 50 meters above ground, the observatory's deck offers views of the fairgrounds that come alive like the snowy scenes in northern countries. This magnificent perspective from above is very peaceful.

  

Even Singapore's Jewel Changi Airport bursts with Christmas cheer. There you can enjoy character-themed decorations and the world's tallest indoor waterfall.

  

As the night winds down and the lights begin to dim, you'll have a full heart - and a new idea of what Christmas can look like.

### Fun Fact

The first Christmas (the birth of Christ) did not take place in a snowy, northern place.

The snowy, winter image of Christmas became globally popular, thanks to advertising by companies like Coca-Cola!

  

The word "cheer" has multiple uses. As a noun it can mean a happy feeling or a shout of gladness. Often, English speakers will give three cheers: "Hip-hip hooray, hip-hip hooray, hip-hip hooray!"
