# Why Can't I keep My New Year's Resolutions?

How to make lasting change

It's almost January 1, and you want to turn over a new leaf. You may have resolved to exercise regularly, study more or quit a bad habit. But there's the problem: Almost 90 percent of New Year's resolutions fall by the wayside within months. Why?

When making your resolutions, you probably made a few mistakes. Maybe your goal was to ambitious, so it never got off the ground. Or you made a resolution because you thought you should - not because you had a strong reason. Because change causes discomfort, you nee solid motivation to stick with it. Or you simply weren't ready. Change requires noticing a need, thinking about it, making a plan, taking action and keeping it up. Without careful planning, your resolution will likely fail.

How can you keep your resolutions? Start small. Choose an important and specific goal with a timeline. Write it down. Make it as visible as possible. Ask a friend to check in on your progress. Then celebrate every small win! Resolutions are met one step at a time.

## Key Point

Successful resolutions require a strong motivation and specific goals supported by planning and visibility.
