# Is Organic Food Better?

Is buying organic worth the price you have to pay?

  

Kara runs into Aiden in the supermarket produce section.

  

Kara: Hey, Aiden! Making your weekly food run?

Aiden: I am! These banana look great, and they're on sale.

  

Kara: But they're not organic bananas. I always buy organic when I have chance.

Aiden: Run-of-the-mill fruits and vegetables are so much more affordable. They're fine with me.

  

Kara: Not with me. Organic ones are better for your health.

Aiden: Really? I've heard there's not a big difference in nutrition.

  

Kara: It's not just about the nutrients in them. It's what's not in them. No farming chemicals or pesticides, and that means they're better for the planet, too.

Aiden: Yeah, but they're also, like, twice the price. My wallet definitely notices!

  

Kara: I see it as an investment - in my body and the planet. I'd rater spend a little more now than deal with health problems later.

Aiden: I think you're being a little dramatic. I feel fine, and I've eaten conventional food my whole life.

  

Kara: Just because you feel fine now doesn't mean those chemicals aren't building up in your body.

Aiden: OK, but there are regulations about how much pesticide farmers are allowed to use. Conventional produce is still safe.

  

Kara: Maybe, but I'd rather be safe than sorry.

Aiden: Don't organic farms still use some pesticides?

  

Kara: Only natural ones, which are usually less harmful. Typical farming mehtods use stuff that kills bees and other wild animals.

Aiden: All right, fair enough. But organic farms raise limited amount of food. To feed everyone, we need conventional commercial farms.

  

Kara: If the demand for organic products increases, more producers will switch.

Aiden: Dream on. As we've discussed, organic is not affordable for many people.

  

Kara: If buying organic were a priority for people, they would find a way to buy it. Also, organic meat and dairy products come from animals treated more humanely. Who isn't in favor of that?

Aiden: Good point. But back to organic products. They seem to spoil faster than conventional ones.

  

Kara: That's because they don't have all the preservatives that regular products have. You just have to buy less and eat it faster. It's about living in a healthier, more responsible way.

Aiden: Well, maybe I'll give organic a try. Just don't expect me to go full out organic overnight.

  

Kara: So, maybe one organic banana at a time?

Aiden: Yeah, maybe.
