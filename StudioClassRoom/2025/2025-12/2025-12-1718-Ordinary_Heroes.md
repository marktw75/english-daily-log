# Ordinary Heroes

Everyday people who make a difference

Many imagine heroes as people with superpowers or grand achievements, yet real heroism often comes from ordinary people. These individuals don't wear capes or leap over tall buildings, but they have a lasting impact through courage, kindness and selflessness. Ordinary heroes are people such as neighbors, teachers, workers and friends who choose to help others even when it's not easy.

One powerful example of an ordinary hero is Malala Yousafzai, a Pakistani education advocate. When Malala was 10, the Taliban took control of the area in which she lived and prohibited girls speeches and blogging about the Taliban's attempts to deny her an education. This resulted in her nomination for the International Children's Peace Prize in 2011. That recognition caught the Taliban's attention, and in 2012 a masked gunman shot her, leaving her in critical condition.

Nine months later, Malala give a speech at the United Nations that focused on education and women's rights. In 2013, she and her father co-founded a non-profit organization that fights for education for girls worldwide.

In 2003, vegetable-seller Chen Si (陳思) had just started his daily walk on the Nanjing Bridge. He saw a man getting ready to jump, so he grabbed him and dragged him back across the bridge railing. From that point on, Si has made it his mission to save others. He spends weekends walking along the bridge trying to prevent people from taking their lives. Si has saved over 400 lives since then.

Phyilis Omido's job at a battery-smelting factory in Africa led her to become an environmental activist. When her son became ill from lead poisoning, she realized it wasn't an isolated case. Quitting her job, she looked into health concerns in her community, finding that many had issues related to lead poisoning. Her campaign for a safe environment got the attention of several international groups, and the factory was closed. It is likely that many lives have been saved since then as nine other plants have also closed.

These ordinary heroes remind us that choosing to help, speak up or care can make a difference.

### Fun Fact

There are different words to describe people who take action to help others (when it is not their job).

Vigilantes protect their community. Volunteers give their time and work for free. Good Samaritans help strangers.

There is a famous saying in English. "Am I my brother's keeper?" A "keeper" is someone who takes care of somethings. A zookeeper cares for animals. We should take care of other people.

## Usage Tip

A + result in + B (lead to, contribute to)

Staying up late can result in/lead to/contribute to having trouble concentrating.

It is likely/unlikely that S + V

It is highly unlikely that such a rare phenomenon would occur twice in a short period of time.
