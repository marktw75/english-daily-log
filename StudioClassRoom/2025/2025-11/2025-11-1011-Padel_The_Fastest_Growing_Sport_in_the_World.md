# Padel: The Fastest-Growing Sport in the World

From a creative family pastime to an international sport

by Christine Bedwell

Padel. It's not tennis. It's not squash. It's something wilder and more fun, meet padel, an action-packed sport! Many versions of its origin exist. But all agree that Mexican millionaire Enrique Corcuera and his wife, Viviana, built the first padel court. A tennis court would not fit on his property in Acapulco, Mexico, so Enrique asked his engineer for a solution. The engineer proposed building a tennis court one-third the standard size on the property's hillside and putting walls on the sides. For years, Enrique had been testing different ways to play tennis on an enclosed court. So, he approved the plan.

With that first court built in 1969, the couple began developing a new sport. Their guests, including princes and celebrities, fell in love with this new racket sport. Padel was easy to learn, fun to play, entertaining to watch and challenging to master. In no time, their guests took padel back to their own courts, spreading the sport to Spain and Viviana's homeland, Argentina. Little did they know that the game they later named padel would become a big hit around the world.

Padel uses solid paddles and a tennis-like ball. In the middle of the padel court is a net, just like in tennis. The court itself is enclosed and smaller than a tennis court. Only underhand serves are allowed because of the court's size. Players let the ball back and forth over the net. But like squash, shots that bounce off the walls are fair game, making the game more dynamic. The scoring is similar to tennis, and the game is usually played in doubles. It's no wonder padel is so popular.

Second only to soccer in popularity in Spain, padel is gaining fans everywhere. The first international padel event, the Corcuera Cup, was held in Mexico in 1991. Today, Premier Padel, formed in 2022, is the top professional tour.

The Corcuera family is still deeply involved. Viviana Corcuera still gives interviews about her husband's invention. Enrique Jr. runs CoPa, a company that honors his father's legacy by producing high-quality padel gear. Interest in the sport is only increasing. A padel court may soon be coming to a location near you!

## Key Points
- In 1969, a space issue led to the Corcuera family's invention of padel, a dynamic sport that quickly spread across continents.
- Padel, a fun mix of tennis and squash, has become a global sport with professional tours like Premier Padel.

## Usage Tip
### Tip 1
For years, S + had been V-ing, ...

過去完成進行式 `had been V-ing` 可用來表示從過去某一時間點之前就開始持續進行，並持續到另一過去時間點的動作。這種用法特別適合描述「長久以來去做某事」的背景。例如：For years, the country had been expanding its territory to increase its power.

### Tip 2
Second only to (soccer) in (popularity)

片語 `second only to` 表示「僅次於……」，其中的 `to` 介系詞之後常接名詞。例如：The school is second to none in science education, boasting a faculty of renowned experts.
