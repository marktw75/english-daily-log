# In Honor Of The Humble Sandwich

Celebrating one of the world's most enjoyable, simple food items

  

What's a meal that's quick and easy to make and fits in your hand? If you said, "a sandwich", you'd be right! Americans will celebrate National Sandwich Day on November 3. It's no wonder America Celebrates the sandwich: Americans eat around 300 million sandwiches a year!

  

Legend has it that the food item's name comes from the Fourth Earl of Sandwich, John Montagu, in the 1700s. The story goes that while playing cards, Montagu ordered two pieces of bread with meat in the middle. Continuing his game, he ate "the sandwich" with one hand, attaching his name to the simple meal forever.

  

Many sandwiches start with a type of bread or a roll and include sauce, meat and vegetables. However, exact ingredients differ from country to country. For example, South Africa's Gatsby Sandwich is loaded with meat, vegetables and a surprise ingredient - French fries! Vietnam's banh mi features meat topped with pickled vegetables and a spicy sauce. If you haven't had a delicious sandwich of the late, grab some bread and let your imagination run wild!

### Fun Fact

Sandwich can be a verb. You can say, "I sandwiched myself between two other people on the bus." To "sandwich" means to fit or squeeze something (or someone) between two other things.
