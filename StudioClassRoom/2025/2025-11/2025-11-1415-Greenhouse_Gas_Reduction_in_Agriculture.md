# Greenhouse Gas Reduction in Agriculture

Growing food without affecting the environment

by Elizabeth Koroma

Farming is an essential industry, yet many experts worry about the greenhouse gases it releases. So, scientists have been researching ways to reduce greenhouse gas emissions without causing food shortages.

## What are Greenhouse Gases?

Greenhouses maintain a warm temperature by trapping heat from the sun, and this warm temperature allows plants to grow when it would otherwise be too cold for them. Greenhouse gases work similarly, except on a global scale. When they are in the atmosphere, they prevent heat from the sun from escaping into space, which, given time, will raise the temperature of the Earth. The widespread and extensive use of coal and oil for fuel has increased the amount of greenhouse gases in the atmosphere over the past few decades. Experts see a global correlation between these levels and rising global temperatures. This has led to a global effort to reduce greenhouse gas emissions, lest the changing climate lead to extreme weather, rising sea levels and other crises.

## Greenhouse Gases and Agriculture

Farmers' choices about what crops to plant can also affect emissions. Between harvest and planting season, many farmers plant cover crops - plants that grow quickly and anchor the soil in place. This also prevents the carbon that is trapped in the soil from being released into the atmosphere. Planting legumes in a field every five years or so can also help since these crops replenish the nitrogen in the soil. After the legumes are harvested, the same field can be used for other crops and will require less fertilizer, resulting in less nitrous oxide in the atmosphere.

Stopping farming altogether is not an option, but the strategic use of resources can reduce the amount of greenhouse gases released in this necessary process.

## Reducing Greenhouse Gas Emissions on Farms

Farmers often need to use techniques that release greenhouse gases, but they can try to make them as effective as possible so they don't need to do them as often. For example, performing regular maintenance on farm equipment can ensure fuel efficiency, which saves expenses on fuel and results in reduced emissions. Some steps in the farming process are more effective at certain times than others. Farmers can calculate the optimal time to run machinery and only use it then.

The agricultural industry typically releases greenhouse gases, but by using resources efficiently and thinking about emissions when deciding which crops they plant, farmers can reduce their greenhouse gas emissions.

## Key Points

- Several processes used in farming release greenhouse gases, which prevent heat from escaping from the Earth and thus raise global temperatures.
- Thoughtful use of resources and techniques can reduce the amount of greenhouse gases released in farming while still allowing farmers to grow crops.
