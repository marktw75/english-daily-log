# A Bunny's Tale

When you know you shouldn't - don't!

It was chilly as Bunny Longears skipped along the trail that led home. Suddenly he halted. He thought he heard someone calling. He looked around but saw nothing. Then he again heard the faint cry. "Please," the voice pleaded, "help me?"

"Oh, dear," thought Bunny. "The forest is full of danger. I should go straight home, but this is so strange. I'd better look into it." So, he headed toward the voice. As he soon approached a deep pit, a cry came from the bottom of the damp hole. "Please," the voice called, "won't you help me?"

"Who are you? What do you want?" asked the curious rabbit. "See for yourself," invited the voice. "I have fallen into this cold, dark ditch. May I look to you for help?"

"I'll have to see about it. Come out where I can look you over," replied Bunny Longears. The creature slithered into view. "I'm just a little garden snake," he grinned slyly.

"I an see right through you," Bunny scolded. "You're no garden snake: You're a cold-blooded killer snake. You're nothing but trouble. Look for help somewhere else." And he dismissed any thought of helping him.

"Wait!" the serpent urged. "One as intelligent as yourself should see fit to help me."

"Intelligent?" smiled the rabbit. "Very!" flattered the snake. "I mean no harm. I only ask a favor."

"What favor?" asked the bunny. "A simple one," replied the crafty serpent. "Just look through the grass and find a strong stick. Then kindly lift me out of this miserable pit. Afterward, we can see one another off ... as friends."

"I really shouldn't", said the bunny, "but maybe I'll look after you this once." So he found a stick and lift the ugly snake out of the pit and onto the grass nearby.

"My!" exclaimed the rabbit as he looed over the snake. "You look disgusting!"

"Yes," grinned the snake. "and you look delicious!"

"Delicious?" cried the rabbit. "But I thought ..."

"Don't be surprised," said the scheming reptile. "You knew I was trouble when you lifted me out of the hole." What happened to the little rabbit I cannot tell. I didn't dare look on. But learn a lesson as you look at this simple tale. When trouble calls - watch out! Keep moving on and don't look back.

### Fun Fact

The word "plea" means a request. It can also be a verb. For example, "I pleaded with my teacher for less homework, but he ignored my plea." This is how we get the word "please" in English. When you say "please" you are making a plea.
