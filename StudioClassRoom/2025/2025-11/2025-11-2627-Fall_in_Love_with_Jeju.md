# Fall in Love with Jeju on Screen and Off

Experience Jeju Island through the seasons

by Christine Bedwell

If you're watching *When Life Gives You Tangerines* on Netflix, you've probably been swept up in the charm of Jeju Island. This emotional K-drama follows Ae-sun and Gwan-sik's love over decades, with Jeju's changing seasons as a striking setting.

Formed by the eruption of Mount Hallasan, Korea's tallest mountain, Jeju is home to three UNESCO World Heritage sites. It's also often called "the Hawaii of South Korea." Just a one-hour flight from Seoul, Jeju, South Korea's largest island, is a favorite spot for both locals and tourists. It's a perfect escape for those seeking adventure, a place to relax or a deeper connection with Korea's natural landscapes.

Each season on Jeju brings something distinct. In spring, fields of yellow flowers near Gasi-ri take your breath away. Enjoy the peace and quiet of a forest or beachside horse ride. Get fired up for the award-winning Jeju Fire Festival on Saebyeol Oreum, a volcanic cone which serves as a stage for light displays, food and the telling of legends. Until a few years ago, grass fields could be seen burning during springtime, a method used to ensure a healthy harvest. Today, the fields no longer burn; however, this festival continues with LED lights, attracting 300,000 visitors a year.

Summer is a great time to explore Jeju's geological phenomena and learn about Jeju's unique community. Step into Manjanggul Cave's lava tubes or study the ancient coral layers along a coastal path near Suwobong Peak. Watch for haenyeo, female divers who harvest seafood deep underwater by holding their breath, instead of using breathing equipment. Then visit the Haenyeo Museum to learn about their unique community and culture.

Walk around the island in the fall on the Jeju Olle Trail. Visit quiet villages. See lava columns at Daepo Jusangjeolli Cliff, hike to Cheonjiyeon Waterfall or dive among the colorful soft coral off Seogwipo. Don't miss the Seogwipo Maeil Olle Market, where you can try tasty black pork and buy souvenirs.

Jeju's winter is a feast for the senses. Bundle up for a snowy hike on Mount Hallasan, or visit Camellia Forest, where red flowers bloom against white snow. Capture sunrise photos at Seongsan Ilchulbong, and pick the island's luscious tangerine-like fruit called hallabong. In the warm up with spicy yellowtail fish soup or pheasant noodles, cherished winter comfort foods of the island.

Whichever season you choose, the sights and experiences on Jeju will stay with you long after you leave.

## Usage Tip

S + could be seen/heard + V-ing

這個句型用來表示某事物曾被觀察到正在進行某個動作。  
例句：The sound of the audience could be heard cheering through the hall.

A feast for the senses

這是一種比喻說法，表示某事帶來豐富、令人愉快的感官體驗。  
例句：The fans feasted their eyes on the rock band during the live performance.
