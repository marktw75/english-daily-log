# Pippi Longstocking Turns 80!

This little red-haired girl is till a role model for young people today

  

Imagine walking out your front door to encounter the strangest child you've ever seen. That's how Annika and Tommy Settergren met their new neighbor, Pippi Lingstocking.

  

With a monkey on her shoulder and a horse on her porch, Pippi was no ordinary girl! Sporting red braids that stuck straight out from her head and wearing shoes twice her show size, Pippi's appearance was alarming. Despite that, the three children struck up an instant friendship, and their adventures began.

  

Since author Astrid Lindgren penned Pippi Longstocking in 1945, children have been fascinated by the strange child. The 9-year-old orphan, who lived alone in a big house, was different from any other child character of the time. With no one tell her what to do, Pippi lived life on her own terms. That characteristic alone probably had many kids wishing for a similar to Pippi's. It was a life defined by her own rules, which reflected her honesty, creativity and care for others. Pippi also exhibits many other virtues that have inspired boys and girls alike over the years.

  

Readers quickly realize that Pippi isn't embarrassed about her appearance or concerned about what others think of her. In a world that pressures kids to fit in, Pippi's confidence and belief in herself are refreshing. Her exploits inspire others to accept their ow unique personalities and be themselves.

  

Shy kids can take heart in the young girl's joy and courageous love of adventure. As she did with Annika and Tommy, Pippi encourages people to move out of their confort zones.

  

In the 1940s, some parents were offended by a strong femal character who relied only on herself. Yet today, this is the very message many parents want their children to adopt.

  

Pippi and her first book turn 80 this year, but her popularity hasn't faded. The little girl with the red braids always confronts challenges head-on and stands up for good. She uses her strength, courage and compassion to improve her world.

  

This year the Astrid Lindgren Company encourages everyone to use their own "Pippi power and make the world a little better."
