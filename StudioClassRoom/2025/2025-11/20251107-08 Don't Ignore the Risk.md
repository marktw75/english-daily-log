# Don't ignore the Risk!

Understanding risk assessment

Risk assessment uses science and data analysis to accurately identify the greatest dangers to the community so that prompt action can be taken to address those dangers.

  

Mary: John, are you really going to smoke that cigarette?

John: Relax! I only smoke once in a while.

Mary: My uncle used to say that. Had he taken the risks of smoking more seriously, he might have avoided getting cancer!

  

Every day, we make decisions that involve risk whether we realize it or not. Although the word "risk" is often associated with the realms of finance and disaster prevention, it also plays an important role in environmental protection. In this context, risk refers to the possibility that exposure to pollutants will cause harm to humans or ecosystems.

  

Risk generally can be classified into two categories: voluntary risks, which individuals willingly undertake, and involuntary risks, which are imposed without individuals' choice. For instance, smoking which significantly heightens the likelihood of cancer, and driving, which brings the risk of traffic accidents, represent voluntary risks because individuals consciously opt to engage in these activities. In contrast, air pollution poses threats to human health, but the general public cannot easily avert exposure to the air they breathe, making this an involuntary risk.

  

Interestingly, people are not always adept at assessing risk. For example, even though many air travelers fear plane crashes, statistics show that flying is far safer than driving. It is this kind of misconception that highlights the need for a specialized process known as risk assessment.
