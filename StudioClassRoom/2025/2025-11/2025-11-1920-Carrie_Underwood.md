# Carrie Underwood

From a small town to the big stage

by Elizabeth Koroma

Carrie Underwood grew up on a farm in Checotah, Oklahoma, an unexpected place to find a future celebrity. And were it not for her family and community there, she likely would never have reached her current success.

Underwood discovered a love for singing in her church choir, and her parents supported this passion in many ways. For example, her mother made many of her costumes for shows by hand. Underwood found her first solo in church around the age of six or seven, and although the experience was scary, she also found joy in it.

Underwood's life changed when she competed on season four of *American Idol*, a singing competition. Underwood was so nervous about performing on a national TV show that she contemplated backing out of her audition. But her parents urged her to go, and she was accepted into the show. In an interview Underwood disclosed, "Every time I had to sing in front of the judges, I'd get nervous the way I did in church the first time. Then I'd say a prayer and leave it in God's hands."

Underwood was crowned the winner of *American Idol* in 2005, which was the beginning of her extraordinary musical career. The show's prize was a record contract, which Underwood used to release her first album. Some Hearts. She followed it up with several singles and additional albums, many of which were bestsellers. Underwood has since won over 100 major music awards and, in 2025, joined the panel of judges on *American Idol*, bringing her career full circle.

In between releasing her first and second albums, Underwood graduated from college. In 2010 she married professional hockey player Mike Fisher, and they have two children. Underwood says she wants to raise her children with the same values she learned.

The singer's other accomplishments include starting a fitness business and writing a book called *Find Your Path* that describes her views on health. *Find Your Path* became an instant bestseller.

Underwood also remembered her hometown, founding the Checotah Animal, Town and School Foundation, C.A.T.S. for short. This organization seeks to meet the community's needs by contributing equipment to schools and running an animal shelter. This is Underwood's way of showing appreciation for the place that enabled her to launch her wildly successful career.

## Key Points

- Carrie Underwood's rise to fame began with singing in church, family support and a life-changing audition on *American Idol*.
- After winning *American Idol*, Underwood released many successful songs and records, won awards, started a business, wrote a book and began a charity to help her hometown.

## Usage Tip

Were it not for + N, S would have + p.p.

這是 `If it were not for ...` 的倒裝形式，用來表達與現在或過去事實相反的條件。  
例句：Had it not been for the scholarship, she wouldn't have been able to study abroad.

S + V + N, many/some/none/all of which ...

這個用法用來補充說明前面的複數名詞。  
例句：I invited ten friends to the party, none of whom showed up on time.
