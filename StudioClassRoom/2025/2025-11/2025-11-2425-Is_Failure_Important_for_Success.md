# Is Failure Important for Success?

Nobody likes to "fail"

by Linda Ludwig

Jessica sees Robert on the bus and sits down beside him.

Jessica: Hey, Robert. Hi! You're looking kind of sad. Anything wrong?

Robert: (deep in thought, Robert finally notices Jessica) Oh, Jessica, hi. Well, I was thinking about my proposal for our office's new workspace. It just got turned down. I feel like such a failure, and I feel as if I've let my team down.

Jessica: Oh, I'm sorry. Do you get another chance to present a different concept?

Robert: Yes. And some suggestions were given, but who cares? I feel like no matter what I do, it's never going to be good enough.

Jessica: I can't tell you not to feel that way right now, but don't let yourself wallow in self-pity too long. When you fail, you learn what doesn't work. That's valuable knowledge you wouldn't have gained otherwise.

Robert: I guess. I did learn a lot from the meeting, but still, it was frustrating and embarrassing to be turned down.

Jessica: It doesn't feel great in the moment, I know. But it builds resilience. Failure is a part of life, so it's your response that really matters.

Robert: That's easy to say, but what if my team keeps failing? It'll kill everyone's confidence.

Jessica: It can - if they let it. But failure doesn't mean you're not capable. It just means you need to try something else.

Robert: It's hard - and even harder to think about how to motivate the team again.

Jessica: That's valid. Fear of failure is real. But try to stay positive and move on - think of it as an opportunity. You've heard of Thomas Edison, right?

Robert: Everybody who uses a light bulb has. Why?

Jessica: There's this quote by him. (looking on her phone) ... Here it is. "I haven't failed 10,000 times. I have succeeded in proving that those 10,000 ways will not work." When I have eliminated the ways that will not work, I will find the way that will work.

Robert: Yeah, someone quoted Steve Jobs after the meeting: "It's impossible to fail if you learn from your mistakes. Don't give up."

Jessica: It's natural to try to avoid failure at all costs. But maybe we need to recognize and appreciate success.

Robert: OK, but it also wastes time, effort and sometimes money. Aren't those downsides?

Jessica: Yes. But when making your next plan, use the feedback you got. Make this failure useful.

Robert: I never thought about it like that. I always focus on the negatives - you know, embarrassment, wasted time and fear.

Jessica: It takes courage to start again, but you won't fail if you never attempt something. Maybe we need to think of failure as not doing anything at all.

Robert: (laughs a little) OK, I guess it's not the end of the world.
