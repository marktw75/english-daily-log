# New in the Neighborhood

The new kid on the block

by Hazel Johansen

It's hard being a porcupine, thought Spike, especially when you're new in town. I don't think anybody likes me. And Spike was right. In fact, the neighbors were already murmuring.

"His hair sticks up," said Possum. "Yes, he sticks out like a sore thumb," frowned Raccoon. "He doesn't belong here," said Beaver. "Well, I heard he's really stuck on the place," snarled Bear. "Yes," said Jack the rabbit. "I heard he wants to stick around."

"Well, try as he may, he'll never fit in. I say we get rid of him," said Weasel. "I've tried," said Badger. "I thought that if I bothered him, he'd leave. Maybe he's decided to stick it out."

"Or maybe he isn't getting the message. One of us will have to tell him," Weasel said. "Who can we stick it to?" asked old Owl. "How about you?" Weasel looked at Turtle. "I'm not sticking my neck out," Turtle said. "Besides, I'm slow ..."

Weasel then fixed his cunning eyes on the rabbit. "You'll tell him, won't you, Jack? You're not slow."

"Yes, I mean, no, I'm not slow, but ..." Jack stammered. "Excellent!" said Weasel. "And stick to your guns. So Jack was stuck with the job.

Spike brightened when he saw the rabbit coming. "Maybe everyone's not stuck-up after all," he thought.

"Hi, I'm new here," he smiled as the rabbit came closer. "Well, that's fine," said Jack. "I mean it's not fine ... you see, nobody likes ... that is, nobody wants ... that is ..."

"I know," sighed Spike. "Nobody likes me. Everybody's waiting for my departure, right?"

"Right!" said the rabbit. "And the sooner the better." Jack started for home, but no sooner had he gone over the hill than he saw a hungry wolf. Sticking out his tongue and licking his lips, the wolf said, "Why, you look tasty."

"This is the end of me!" cried the rabbit. Suddenly Spike appeared. "Don't worry," said the porcupine. "I'll stick by you." And he stepped between the rabbit and the wolf.

"Don't stick your nose in where it doesn't belong!" growled the wolf. When the wolf went after him, the porcupine turned, and his long quills stuck into the wolf's face. "Ow! Ow!" shrieked the wolf as he fled.

The rabbit sat trembling. Finally, the porcupine said, "Time for me to go."

"Please stay," smiled Jack. "The longer the better." So Spike stayed, and the two became close companions - always sticking together and always sticking up for one another.

Reaching out to others is sometimes difficult - especially if they're different. But stick with it. Maybe you'll find a friend with some good points.

## Usage Tip
### Tip 1
`Try as he may`

慣用語 `try as one may/might` 意為「儘管某人再怎麼努力」，其後接主要子句以表達結果不如預期。例句：Try as the doctor might, he couldn't save the patient's life.

### Tip 2
`No sooner had + S + p.p. ... than ...`

此為倒裝句型，用於表示「一……就……」，主詞與助動詞位置倒裝。`No sooner` 置句首時，需用 `had + p.p.` 表示前一動作已完成，後方常接 `than` 引導另一事件。例句：No sooner had the concert tickets gone on sale than they were sold out.
