# Egypt: Land of Wonders

An exciting glimpse into history

by Cindy Busch, edited by Linda Ludwig

After anticipating a trip to Egypt for over a year, my friend Laurie and I finally set foot on Egyptian soil. The country's long history, ancient culture and famous landmarks had held our interest for years.

Our hotel not only overlooked the immense city of Cairo but also provided magnificent views of the Nile River. In Cairo, a lively city of 22.6 million, we explored the Cairo Citadel, Alabaster Mosque and the Egyptian Museum. One evening, we walked through Khan el-Khalili, Cairo's oldest market, where we shopped and enjoyed traditional Egyptian dishes. The desserts, especially the baklava, were my favorites! Likewise, the falafel and baba ganoush were delicious.

Outside the hubbub of Cairo, we rode camels around the famous Pyramids of Giza, built between 2551 and 2472 B.C. We swayed back and forth on our camels as they slowly walked among the gigantic structures. At least 118 pyramids, pharaohs' burial sites, are here; several of which are among the largest structures ever built. The 4,600-year-old Great Pyramid of Khufu is the last of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World in existence. Seeing the pyramids from the back of a camel on the sands of the Sahara Desert was an unforgettable experience!

After Cairo, we flew to Luxor, where we boarded a ship and cruised on the upper Nile River. On the Nile's east bank, we visited the Luxor Temple and the 3000-year-old Temple of Karnak, the largest religious building ever constructed. It was amazing to see that the hieroglyphs on the walls were still readable.

On the Nile's west bank, we toured the world-famous archeological site the Valley of the Kings. Since some of the 63 tombs of Egypt's great pharaohs from the New Kingdom, such as Ramses, are open, we had a look inside several.

Sailing farther south, we arrived at the Valley of the Queens, where queens, princesses and other officials of the New Kingdom were buried. We explored Queen Nefertari's tomb, one of the area's largest and most impressive tombs, with its colorful artwork.

Farther south, we took in the famous Aswan Dam, which controls the annual Nile flooding, and a Nubian village to experience people's lives in southern Egypt.

Back in Cairo, we came face to face with the mummies of the ancient Kings and Queens of Egypt on display at the National Museum of Egyptian Civilization.

My trip to Egypt was all that I had hoped it would be - and more!

## Key Points

- Our trip to Egypt was an unforgettable experience from exploring Cairo, wandering through rich local culture and riding camels around the Pyramids of Giza to exploring ancient tombs.
- We explored Egypt's ancient history and enjoyed the Nile River, from cruising along the Nile to visiting temples, tombs and villages.

## Usage Tip
### Tip 1
`S1 + V1, likewise, S2 + V2`

副詞 `likewise` 表 similarly 與「同樣地」，可用於補充前句的描述，表示 S1 與 S2 有相似情況。例句：The baklava and baba ganoush were likewise delicious.

### Tip 2
`all that I had hoped it would be`

本用法中的 `all that I had hoped it would be` 直譯為「所有我所希望的樣子」，可用於表達事物完全符合期待，甚至超越預期。例句：The movie was all that I had hoped it would be - exciting, gripping and inspiring.
