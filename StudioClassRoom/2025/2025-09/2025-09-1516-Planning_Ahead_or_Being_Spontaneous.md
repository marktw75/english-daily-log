# Planning Ahead or Being Spontaneous

Is it better to have a plan or be flexible?

by Ruth DeVine

Levi and Jordan run into each other at a coffee shop.

Jordan: Hey, Levi, what are you working on?

Levi: I'm planning out my schedule for next month.

Jordan: Why? You don't know what's going to happen next month.

Levi: I know, but if I don't make a plan, nothing will!

Jordan: You have a tendency to overthink things. What you need to do is relax and smell the roses a little more, my friend.

Levi: And with some careful planning, I can do just that. It'll save me time, reduce my stress and help me make sure things go well. If you don't plan, you could end up wasting time or missing out on things.

Jordan: That's where we totally differ. Being spontaneous is so much better. Planning too much takes the fun out of things. Sometimes the best experiences happen when you just go with the flow.

Levi: OK, let's take traveling. If you don't plan ahead, you might not get good hotel deals or tickets to popular attractions. Just imagine showing up in Paris with no hotel reservation and everything is booked!

Jordan: I think some of the best travel experiences happen when you explore without a strict schedule. You could find a cool little Airbnb or discover a restaurant you didn't look up online. Plus, last-minute deals can sometimes be cheaper.

Levi: OK, but what about a party? If you don't plan, you might run out of food or end up with no music. You have to do some planning if you want to make sure people have a great time.

Jordan: Not necessarily. Parties planned to the nth degree can feel stiff and boring. Some great parties happen when people just get together without any real plan. Someone might bring an unexpected game, or a new friend might show up. That's what makes a party fun.

Levi: Fine, but if you're going somewhere, say an amusement park, planning is key. If you don't check the best times to go, you have to endure waiting in long lines instead of having fun.

Jordan: But if you don't plan, you're not stuck to a rigid schedule. You can decide then and there what rides look fun instead of stressing out about hitting all the attractions on your list. It'll be more relaxing and enjoyable.

Levi: I get that, but having a plan at least gives you some direction.

Jordan: And I think it's more exciting to just see what will happen. A mix is probably best, but I'll always lean toward being spontaneous!

Levi: And I'll always prefer having a plan!

## Usage Tip
### Tip 1
`What you need to do is + (to) V`

本句型用來表達「某人需要做的事情是……」。`What + 子句` 會形成名詞子句，作為句子主詞；`is` 後方接 `to V` 或原形動詞，作為補語說明具體內容。例句：What you need to do is (to) proofread the paper before submitting it.

### Tip 2
`then and there` vs. `here and now`

慣用語 `then and there` 字面意思為「在那時當場」，語意指當下就要做出行動；`here and now` 則是用於表達「此時此地」，強調現在就開始處理。例句：We should call the police here and now to report the theft.
