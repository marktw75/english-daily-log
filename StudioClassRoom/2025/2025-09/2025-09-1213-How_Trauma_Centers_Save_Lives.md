# How Trauma Centers Save Lives

Learn more about the heroes in this important medical unit

by Pamela Osment

Against a background of bombs, car crashes and explosions, Dr. Baek Kang-hyuk brings life-saving medicine to a hospital in the chaos of a war zone.

This exciting, white-knuckle opening scene from The Trauma Code: Heroes on Call grabs attention immediately. The K-drama tops the list of popular TV shows in more than 60 countries, telling the story of a genius surgeon who has been working in conflict zones around the world. Then he is recruited to bring a trauma team up to a satisfactory level at a university hospital that is experiencing financial strain.

Some hospitals have units that are equipped with people who are trained to provide emergency life-saving care for those with critical injuries.

Real-life trauma centers also experience heart-stopping action and are credited with saving many lives around the world each year. Sadly, there are too few of these crucial units globally to meet the needs of critical care.

According to the World Health Organization (WHO), over 4.4 million deaths every year are attributed to injuries caused by trauma. A 2020 study determined that over 2 million lives could be saved each year through a trauma system, which is an organized effort designed to deliver a full range of medical services. These are typically provided by medical professionals in a trauma unit with special training. A trauma unit specializes in treating emergency patients with critical injuries that go beyond the capabilities of a regular emergency room.

Though trauma centers are often located in a hospital's emergency department, they are more than basic emergency rooms. Trauma units are the equivalent of rapid response teams (RRTs) that are trained to treat patients immediately upon arrival at the hospital. They typically administer care for extreme cases such as gunshot and stab wounds, car crash injuries, brain injuries and more. Their facilities are equipped with equipment designed to handle critical injuries.

Trauma centers are typically divided into five levels that indicate the type of resources that are available.

Level I centers provide 24-hour care by surgeons and specialists who provide care for every aspect of an injury, from prevention to rehabilitation.

Level II centers can begin treatment, but they might not be able to offer comprehensive care.

Level III trauma centers are capable of assessing and stabilizing the patient, often performing surgery and intensive care.

Level IV units have the ability to evaluate, stabilize and administer life support before transferring patients to a higher-level trauma center.

Level V units can stabilize the patient and diagnose the problem before transferring the person to an upper-level facility.

Combining speed, expertise and technology, trauma team specialists act when minutes matter and lives are on the line.

## Key Points

- Trauma centers play a crucial role in saving lives, but there are not enough of them to meet the need for emergency medical care.
- Trauma centers, classified into five levels based on their resources and capabilities, provide life-saving care for critical injuries, combining speed, expertise and advanced technology to treat patients.

## Usage Tip
### Tip 1
`be designed to + V` vs. `be designed for + N`

`be designed to + V` 用來表功能、能力或目的；`be designed for + N` 則用來表對象或用途。本句的 trauma system is designed to deliver a full range of medical services，便是在說明其目的。例句：The introductory course is designed to teach beginners.

### Tip 2
`the equivalent of vs. be equivalent to`

表「相等」作名詞時，需搭配 `A be the equivalent of B`；表 `A 與 B` 具有功能、價值或效果上相似時，則可用形容詞結構 `be equivalent to`。例句：The harm caused by the air pollution is the equivalent of smoking a pack of cigarettes daily.
