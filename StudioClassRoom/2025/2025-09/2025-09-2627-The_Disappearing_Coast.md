# The Disappearing Coast

Slowing erosion and preserving our shores

by Christine Bedwell

Erosion along coasts, caused by waves, wind and human activity, can lead to flooding, land loss and environmental damage. Preserving natural barriers and improving regulations can slow erosion and protect communities.

Have you ever wondered at the mysterious landforms that are scattered along coastlines? Caves, arches and tall pillars called sea stacks form through erosion, a process in which water and wind wear away the land over time. These whimsical landforms invite the adventurous to explore a sea cave at low tide, float through natural arches or photograph the sun setting behind a lone sea stack.

Though formed over centuries by relentless waves and wind, these breathtaking landforms are fragile. The same forces that created them will wear them away, changing the coastline once again. Coastal erosion is natural, but human activity is accelerating it, threatening not just these formations but also coastal communities and ecosystems.

Climate change, which is caused by pollution and emissions, makes coastal erosion worse. Stronger storms damage coastlines, and rising sea levels flood areas that were safe at one time.

Coastal erosion harms both people and the environment. As land disappears, habitats for plants and animals are lost. Wetlands, which filter the water, store carbon, prevent flooding and shelter diverse species, shrink or vanish. Coral reefs, mangrove forests and seaweed beds, which also help to slow climate change through absorbing carbon and marine life, are damaged or destroyed. This loss reduces the diversity of plants and animals and disrupts ecosystems, making survival harder for fish and other species. It also threatens local communities that depend on fishing and tourism to make a living.

To reduce erosion, people must restore natural barriers. Protecting and/or rebuilding coral reefs, mangroves and seaweed beds can help shield coastlines. Campaigns to educate the public about human-caused erosion as well as stronger laws can help prevent harmful coastal development. In highly populated areas, well-planned dams and sea walls can offer protection.

Healthy coastlines are essential for regulating the climate, providing protection from storms and sustaining future generations. Only by supporting conservation efforts, making sustainable choices and advocating for better policies can we restore our coastlines and preserve their incredible landscapes. By protecting our coastlines, we can ensure that future generations will walk on the same shores, explore the same sea caves and gaze at the beauty of our ever-changing coasts.

## Key Points

- Erosion creates landforms through natural forces, but human activity, such as removing natural barriers, speeds up erosion, causing habitat loss.
- People must support conservation efforts to restore natural defenses that protect coastal habitats and advocate for better policies to protect these ecosystems for future generations.

## Usage Tip
### Tip 1
`Nonrestrictive clause`

非限定關係子句為關係代名詞（如 `which`）所引導，用以補充前面的先行詞並非必要資訊。句中 `which is caused by pollution and emissions` 就是在補充說明 climate change。非限定關係子句前後通常以逗點隔開。例句：Leonardo da Vinci, who painted the Mona Lisa, was not only an artist but also a scientist.

### Tip 2
`Only ... by + V-ing`

`only by` 用來強調唯有透過某方法才能達成某結果。後面通常接動名詞，說明手段或方式。例句：Only when you push your fears are you able to grow beyond them.
