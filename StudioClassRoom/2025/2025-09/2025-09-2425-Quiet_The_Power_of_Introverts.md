# Quiet: The Power of Introverts in a World That Can't Stop Talking

Silence can be power

summarized by Simon Stopps

Many believe that being social and talkative is the key to success. Schools, workplaces and social events often reward those who speak up, take charge and enjoy being around others. But not everyone is good at those things. In Quiet: The Power of Introverts in a World That Can't Stop Talking, Susan Cain explores the strengths of introverts. She explains how modern culture often overlooks quiet and thoughtful people even though they have unique abilities that help society.

It has been argued that many workplaces and schools are designed for extroverts. Group projects, open offices and team-based activities encourage talking and interaction, while research in psychology shows that introverts work best in quiet spaces. They focus deeply, think carefully before speaking and often come up with creative ideas.

Cain explains that neither personality type is better. Rather, both have strengths that balance each other out. The book notes that introverts are often good listeners, ask that can potentially make them great leaders, friends and problem solvers. She shares research that shows some of history's influential figures, such as Albert Einstein, were introverts.

Throughout the book, Cain introduces successful introverts who have impacted society. She describes Bill Gates, a quiet but highly analytical thinker, and Dr. Seuss, who preferred solitude when writing his famous children's books. These examples show that introverts can achieve great success without becoming outgoing. That is why Cain encourages introverts to embrace their natural strengths rather than trying to be extroverted.

Cain also gives advice for introverts and the people around them. She encourages introverts to find environments that suit their needs, such as alternative workspaces or flexible classrooms. For parents and teachers, she suggests allowing children time to work alone instead of always pushing them to join group activities. Employers can benefit by giving workers the option of working independently. These changes help introverts perform at their best.

Overall, Quiet contests the idea that being social and outgoing is always best. Helping introverts understand themselves better and teaching extroverts how to appreciate different personality types is best. Cain's message is clear: The world needs both introverts and extroverts, and success is not about changing who you are but about using your natural strengths.

## Key Points

- Susan Cain's book Quiet explores the strengths of introverts and introvert's contributions, how introverts thrive in thoughtful, quiet environments.
- The book Quiet emphasizes that introverts and extroverts both support society, and introverts can achieve success by creating environments that support their strengths.

## Usage Tip
### Tip 1
`It has been + p.p. + that`

本句型用於間接引述，以虛主詞 `It` 帶出後文的 `that` 子句，並以被動語態呈現某種「已被指出／宣稱／揭露」的概念。常搭配 `claim`、`reveal`、`report` 等動詞使用。例句：It has been revealed that several celebrities were involved in the scandal.

### Tip 2
`S + suggest + V-ing/that + S2 + (should) + VR`

動詞 `suggest` 有「建議；表示」之意。本判別語意上可依前後文看出，若後方接 `V-ing` 表示說話者建議某個做法；若後方接 `that` 子句，子句中的主詞、動詞可搭配 `should + VR` 呈現。句中 `she suggests allowing children time to work alone` 為動名詞用法。例句：The speaker suggested taking (that the audience should) take notes during the presentation.
