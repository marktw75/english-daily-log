# How Sports Events Are Going Green

Greener games for a cleaner future

by David Morton

Sports organizations are increasingly striving to lower their environmental impact during sports events by reducing waste, using sustainable materials, sourcing local food and promoting eco-friendly transportation.

Whether it's a local match or a global competition like the Olympics, sports events create excitement, energy and a sense of unity. However, while these events captivate millions, little do most people realize their significant environmental footprint. Large crowds, energy-intensive facilities and the use of disposable plastic and paper products contribute to pollution and waste. Fortunately, some sports organizations are now embracing sustainable practices to reduce their impact on the planet.

The 2024 Paris Olympics is a prime example of an eco-friendly event. With a strong focus on sustainability, the Olympics aimed to reduce carbon emissions, promote zero waste and encourage low-carbon transportation. Taiwan has also taken action. Since 2024, its Ministry of Environment has collaborated with major sports organizers to implement plastic reduction and low-carbon strategies. The goal is to make sports events a vehicle for environmental education, thereby changing people's daily habits.

One of the most effective ways to make a sports event more sustainable is by reducing single-use items. Event organizers can digitize materials such as brochures and replace printed posters with LED screens. In Japan, in an effort to cut down on plastic waste at professional baseball games, fans can enjoy "plastic-free viewing" by bringing their own water bottles with sponsors providing reusable eco-friendly cups.

But sustainability extends beyond paper and single-use plastics. The 2020 Tokyo Olympic medals were made from recycled electronic devices, while the 2024 Paris Olympic medals featured iron fragments from the Eiffel Tower.

Another key area for improvement is food consumption. In the past, sports venues sourced food from distant suppliers, leading to high carbon emissions from transportation. Today, more events focus on food-waste reduction and low-carbon dining by using local ingredients and partnering with food banks to donate unused food products, lest excess food go to waste.

Athletes and spectators also play a role in making sports more sustainable. For athletes, using reusable protective gear and water bottles can help reduce waste while spectators can cut down on carbon emissions by taking public transportation, cycling or walking to sports venues.

The design of the venue itself must also be considered, with many new stadiums being built with sustainability in mind. Installing solar panels for clean energy, using energy-efficient lighting and adopting smart energy management systems (SEMS) all help lower electricity use. Additionally, natural ventilation and lighting can reduce the need for air-conditioning and electric lights, further supporting energy conservation and carbon reduction.

Sporting events are not just about rivalry - they are also a platform for raising awareness and inspiring change. By integrating eco-friendly principles into sporting events, athletes, organizers and fans can work together to create a more sustainable future. With every step taken toward greener sports, we move closer to a world where both athletic excellence and environmental responsibility go hand in hand.

## Key Points

- Sports events are promoting sustainable practices by reducing carbon emissions, using recyclable materials and making events an opportunity for environmental education.
- Athletes and spectators can make sports events greener by not using single-use plastic items, and venues can help by using ventilation systems that reduce the need for electricity.

## Usage Tip
### Tip 1
`Little do + S + V`

倒裝句，用來加強語氣，用來強調「幾乎不知道」，也呼應前方的否定意味。句子原本的助動詞或 be 動詞需提前到主詞之前。例句：Little did she realize how much her words had hurt him.

### Tip 2
`lest + S + (should) + VR`

連詞 `lest` 表「以免，免得」，後方引導目的子句，表示為避免某情況發生。其後可接 `that` 子句或直接接原形動詞；現代英文裡常見結構為 `lest + S + should + VR`，也可省略 `should`。例句：I packed an umbrella lest it rain.
