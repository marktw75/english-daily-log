# Bouldering

Climbing without ropes

by Simon Stopps

If you've ever wanted to climb like Spider-Man but without the responsibility of saving the city, try bouldering. Unlike traditional rock climbing, this sport is about climbing short but tricky walls or rocks without any ropes. It's a test of one's strength and problem-solving skills. It's also enjoyable for climbers of all levels, so it's becoming more and more popular worldwide.

Bouldering can be enjoyed inside or outside. Traditional gyms provide environments with walls of various difficulties, often featuring colorful climbing holds, making them good places for beginners. Outdoor bouldering is more challenging as climbers go up natural rock formations and encounter conditions that may change quickly. While bouldering inside a gym is fun, outdoor climbing is an adventure in nature.

To start bouldering, you'll need climbing shoes for grip and protection and a crash pad for falls. Most gyms rent these items, while outdoor climbers need their own gear. A bag of climbing chalk to keep your hands dry is also helpful. And don't forget water and snacks.

While bouldering may not take you hundreds of feet into the sky, safety is a priority! A good climbing partner, or spotter, is essential. They'll help guide your movements and make sure you land on your crash pad - not the hard ground! If you're climbing outdoors, watch the weather because wet rocks can turn into slippery ones.

Footwork is everything! Using your legs takes pressure off your arms, making each move smoother and less tiring. Grip is another game-changer - keep your hands open rather than grasping them like you're holding onto your last piece of pizza. Experienced climbers also rely on their core to stay balanced and stable, helping them stay on the wall longer. Start small, build confidence and engage in harder climbs as you improve!

Bouldering is like solving a puzzle with your body. Every climb challenges your strength, creativity and ability to remain flexible. One moment you're gracefully grasping onto tiny holds, the next, you're holding onto the wall like a desperate cat avoiding a bath. But that's the fun of it!

## Key Points

- Enjoyed indoors or outdoors, bouldering is a fun climbing sport that requires strength, problem-solving skills and gear like climbing shoes, chalk and crash pads.
- Bouldering requires proper technique and footwork to climb safely.

## Usage Tip
### Tip 1
`S1 + V1 ..., while/whereas S2 + V2`

從屬連接詞 `while` 與 `whereas` 均有「而……的同時／與……相反」之意，可用於比較兩種情況。句中將 gym climbers 與 outdoor climbers 置於同一結構下做對比。例句：While/Whereas plants produce oxygen, animals consume it.

### Tip 2
`Simile`

`as ... as` 與 `like` 可用來做比較，形成明喻（simile）修辭。句中用 `like you're holding onto your last piece of pizza` 來形象地說明手部緊握的感覺。例句：As busy as a bee. / Just as a lighthouse guides ships in the dark, a good teacher leads students through confusion.
