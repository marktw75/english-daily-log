# Are Physical or Online Bookstores Better?

Where is the best place to buy books?

by Ruth Devlin

Jake is reading, and Emma comes and sits beside him.

**Emma:** I see you have your nose in a book again, Jake.

**Jake:** I do! I bought this novel online as soon as it came out.

**Emma:** So, you prefer buying books online to getting them from a physical bookstore?

**Jake:** Totally. Buying online is fast, easy and there are way more options.

**Emma:** But I love physical bookstores. You can hold the book, flip through the pages and even discover new ones you weren't looking for.

**Jake:** I know. But for me it's all about the **convenience**. You don't have to leave your house.

**Emma:** Still, bookstores let you get the book right away — no waiting for shipping. And the atmosphere is cool. Sometimes they even have reading areas or special events.

**Jake:** Yeah, the **vibe** is cool. But if they don't have the book in stock, you have to wait anyway.

**Emma:** But that doesn't happen very often, and shopping in person helps support local businesses. That's important, too.

**Jake:** Definitely. But online stores sometimes have cheaper digital versions or used copies, which save money.

**Emma:** I guess both have benefits. There's no denying that shopping online is convenient, but bookstores offer a better experience.

**Jake:** Agreed. Buy online when it's **urgent**, and go to a bookstore when you want to explore!

## Word Bank

## Usage Tip
### Tip 1
Prefer ... to ...

動詞 prefer 意為「較喜歡」，用來指出某人的偏好。當搭配介系詞 to，形成 prefer A to B，以表「比起 B，更喜歡 A」，在語義上藉由兩個對等結構來保持平衡。例句：I prefer teamwork to independent work because it allows for idea exchange.
