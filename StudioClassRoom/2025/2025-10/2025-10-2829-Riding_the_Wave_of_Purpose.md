# Riding the Wave of Purpose

How one woman is defying expectations and paving the way for others to follow

At 46, Kimberly Chen is proving that it's never too late to pursue your dreams. A successful business owner and mother of two, she is also a champion wake surfer. Chen didn't participate in a lot of sports growing up, and it wasn't until she was in her 30s that she even discovered wake surfing. What began as a weekend hobby with her family quickly became a life-changing passion.

In spite of spending more time in the water than on her wake surfeboard during her first lesson, something clicked, and she was **hooked**.

Chen soon began entering competitions, rising through the ranks with great speed. By 2019, she had earned second place in the Women's Masters Division at the World Wake Association (WWA) **Championships**. From 2020 to 2023, she ranked number one in the Masters Women's Surf Division of the Competitive Wake Surf Association (CWSA). However, her victories didn't come without criticism. Some questioned her age, her motives and even her ability. Others told her that her goals weren't **realistic**. But Chen stayed focused.

"I wanted my children to see what determination looks like," she said. Her story is a powerful reminder that **accomplishment** has no age limit.

Kimberly Chen is more than a champion wake surfer — she's also a **fitness** coach, author and a role model. With over 200,000 followers on social media, she shares tips to encourage others to live strong, active lives. She also teaches young girls to succeed in water sports and overcome their fears. She often hugs them when they come out of the water, saying that they never thought they could do it.

She believes people have immense potential to turn their dreams into reality. Her book, 不妥協的美麗 (*Beauty With No Boundary*), tells the story of how she went from being a businesswoman and mom to a world-class athlete. Her daughter, Jocelyn, a successful wake surfer in her own right, has an affectionate nickname for Mom — Kim Possible. This name highlights Chen's **remarkable** ability to **accomplish** whatever she sets her mind on doing.

Chen often shares stories to inspire others. One of her favorites is about her dad, who began wake surfing at almost 60. His message is simple: **Regardless** of your age or background, it's never too late to begin again.

Kimberly Chen isn't just making waves on the water — she's helping others **abandon** fear, believe in themselves and live without limits.

## Key Points
- Kimberly Chen started wake surfing in her 30s and quickly became a world champion, proving that it's never too late to pursue your dreams.
- As a fitness coach and author, she inspires others to overcome their fears, achieve their goals and believe in their potential.

## Usage Tip
### Tip 1
What began as + N + V...

本句型中的 what 引導名詞子句作主詞，表示「一開始是⋯⋯的事物」，由於是在說明某事物以前的狀態，子句中的動詞以過去式 began 呈現。此結構常用來引出某事物隨時間改變的前後差異，因此後主詞搭配的動詞常具有「轉變」之意，如 become, turn into, evolve into 等。例句：What began as a local protest evolved into a nationwide movement.

### Tip 2
A is more than B

慣用語 more than 表「不只是⋯⋯而已」，意即與 not only, not just, not merely 相近，後面常接名詞或形容詞，用來強調某人或某物具有的身份或狀態不僅於此。如本句即表示，Kimberly Chen 有快艇衝浪冠軍之外的身分，包含健身教練、作家、榜樣等等。例句：The speech was more than emotional — it inspired real change.
