# Autumn Activity

Don't let laziness be your downfall

by Hazel Johansen

Leaves covered the forest floor. "Autumn is a time of activity," **chattered** Papa Squirrel to his little ones. "Now is the time to collect nuts, seeds and berries for winter. Our food supply falls off as the weather grows colder."

"Can we help, too?" asked the youngsters. "Oh yes!" exclaimed their father. "It's an **immense** task."

"Can I help, too?" a black crow cawed from the trees. "Why?" asked Papa Squirrel. "Fly south for the winter along with the other crows."

"I was going to," the crow **sobbed**. "But I fell out with the other crows, and they left without me. Since my plans fell through, my world is falling apart," he cried. "Can't I help you gather food? Then we can share it when winter arrives. I'll work really hard."

"Perhaps we can use an extra hand, uh, wing, that is," Papa Squirrel agreed. "Fall in with the rest of the family."

"I'll get some berries," shouted the first little squirrel. "I'll find some seeds," cried the second. "I'll look for nuts," chattered the third.

"How are you going to help?" Papa Squirrel asked the crow. "I'll **oversee** the work," he answered. "I'll make sure no one falls behind."

So, the squirrel family gathered and stored food for the winter. Not one **scrap** fell by the wayside.

As the days grew colder, the squirrels worked harder, but the lazy crow only **criticized**. Finally, the work was complete. "Everything has fallen into place," boasted the **idle** crow. "We've squirreled away more than enough food. Now let's enjoy these berries while they're fresh and delicious."

"Sounds good to me," shouted the first little squirrel. "Me, too," cried the second. "Me, three," laughed the third.

But their father shook his head. "Don't fall for the advice of this lazy old crow," he warned. "Winter may be long. If we fall short of seeds and nuts, we'll need these berries to fall back on."

"You're right," agreed the little squirrels. Papa Squirrel said, "Now run along, it's nap time." The little squirrels hurried to their **cozy** home, where they fell asleep.

"I guess I **deserve** a nap, too," **yawned** the crow. "I'm extremely tired from watching everyone work so hard. Wake me when it's time to eat."

Papa Squirrel shook his head once more. "Laziness is your downfall," he **scolded**. "You fell all over yourself to help, but you fell down on the job. Now don't expect us to help you." And the lazy crow was left out in the cold.

Don't expect your share of the blessings if you don't do your share of the work.

## Usage Tip
### Tip 1
That is

慣用語 that is 亦可寫為 that is to say，意為「也就是說，更精確地說」，用於對前述內容做出更細節更貼切的解說。如本句，由於烏鴉的手就是翅膀，或說烏鴉沒有手，說是把 hand 修正為 wing，讓讀者更清楚知道此為兩個相同概念。例句：He's a dermatologist — that is, a doctor who treats skin diseases.

### Tip 2
Deserve + N/to V/V-ing

動詞 deserve 意為「值得、應得」，用於表達某人或某物當獲得某種回應、報償或處罰。Deserve 可後接名詞，亦可接不定詞 to V，表「應得某結果」，如 the corrupt manager deserved to lose his job；若接動名詞 V-ing，主詞通常為事物，且具被動意涵，表「某事值得被⋯⋯」。例句：The ocean's mysteries deserve exploration/to be explored/exploring.
