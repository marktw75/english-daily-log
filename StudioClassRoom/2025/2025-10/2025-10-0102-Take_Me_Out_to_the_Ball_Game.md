# Take Me Out to the Ball Game

This **timeless** sport builds community around the world

by Christian Bednari

Once considered America's **pastime**, baseball has also become a national sport in parts of South America and Asia.

Bat-and-ball games **date back** to ancient Egypt. But in 1845, the New York Knickerbocker Club **stepped up to the plate** and laid the foundation for modern baseball. They **replaced** an open field with a diamond and established fixed rules. They also introduced the idea of three strikes and got rid of throwing the ball at runners to get them out.

Though modern baseball started in North America, players around the world have made baseball history. Japanese player Shohei Ohtani, who plays for the L.A. Dodgers, is compared to the unforgettable Babe Ruth. Babe Ruth was famous in the 1920s and 30s for his pitching ability and his **home runs**. And in 2023, Venezuelan Ronald Acuña Jr. became the first player to hit over 40 home runs and **steal** more than 70 bases. His power and speed **remind** baseball fans of American Baseball Hall of Fame's Rickey Henderson — he's truly **covering all the bases**. These players show that baseball knows no borders.

Team Taiwan made history last November when it won its first high-level international baseball tournament at the WBSC Premier12 championship against Japan. More recently, Taiwan secured its spot for the 2026 World Baseball Classic (WBC) after beating Spain.

Baseball is more than a game — it's a tradition that brings people together. Whether in the U.S., Japan or Cuba, fans share a love for the sport. Writer Alex Chesse calls it a "chess match in motion" because of the **strategy** used in every play, making it exciting for players and fans alike.

The social aspect is just as important. American fans gather to tailgate, barbecuing and **hanging out** before the game. In Japan, crowds **energize** their teams with chants, songs and dances. And in Cuba, the young and the **elderly** bond over *pelota de barrio*; local games played for fun.

Baseball connects people across cultures. American teacher Horace Wilson introduced it to Japan in the 1870s. Then in 1915, the Japanese popularized it in Taiwan. Cuban students in America **hit it out of the park** when they took baseball home to Cuba with them. Baseball's **appeal** spread quickly and soon became the island's national sport. Looking to the future, the 2026 World Baseball Classic will feature 20 participating countries.

Whether people play or watch, baseball bonds people and nations.

## Key Points
- Baseball started in the U.S., but players like Shohei Ohtani and Ronald Acuña Jr. have become international stars of the sport.
- Baseball brings people together from around the world through fun, tradition and a shared love for the game.

## Usage Tip
### Tip 1
Once considered + SC (N/adj.), S + present tense

的 once 意為「曾經，一度」，後接過去分詞 considered，再接主補語（以名詞或形容詞呈現），是為指出主詞過去被認為具有的身分或特質，主要子句則說明主詞的現況。因此，時態通常使用現在簡單式或現在完成式。本句型主要用於比較某人或某事物的今昔狀態。例句：Once considered reliable, the data no longer reflect current market trends.

### Tip 2
Whether ... or ..., S + V

從屬連接詞 whether 可引導讓步的副詞子句，搭配對等連接詞 or，連接兩個以上的選項，表達「不管哪個狀況，主要子句內容仍舊成立」。不管 whether 後接介系詞片語 in the U.S. 還是子句，是因應主要子句共用主詞。例句：Whether you take online courses or attend in-person classes, concentration is the key to success.
