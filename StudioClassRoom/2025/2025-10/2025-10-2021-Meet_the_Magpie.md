# Meet the Magpie

These fascinating birds are full of surprises

by Ruth Devlin

Maybe you've seen a bold black-and-white bird with a long tail **strut** across the grass. Or you've seen one hop along the top of a wall. Or maybe you've heard birds in a tree "talking" up a storm. If so, chances are you've had a magpie encounter.

Known for their **curiosity** and striking looks, magpies are some of the most fascinating birds in the world. And no — they don't steal shiny things as often as people say, but they sure do love attention!

The name magpie comes from Old English. "Mag" was a **nickname** for Margaret and was used for women who liked to talk. Since these birds are loud and chatty, the name stuck like glue! "Pie" referred to their black-and-white coloring. So, magpie basically means a noisy black-and-white bird — and that fits perfectly.

Magpies belong to the same family as crows and ravens. With its familiar **elegant** black-and-white coloring, the Eurasian magpie is the most well-known. But there are about a dozen kinds, including the Korean magpie, which is the national bird of Korea.

Found all over the world, magpies have **adapted** to living in forests, grasslands and even cities. They eat just about anything: insects, fruit, seeds and animals as well as dead ones. They are so smart that they can recognize themselves in mirrors — the only bird who can do so. And they solve puzzles as well as show signs of future planning. Some have even passed the famous "marshmallow test," which measures **self-control**.

Magpies build large, dome-shaped nests and often mate for life. During breeding season, they can get **aggressive** and protect their nests — even attacking people who get too close.

In China, magpies are a symbol of happiness and good fortune. People in Tibet consider them birds of joy. But in European stories, a single magpie might be a sign of bad luck.

Magpies are not only smart — they're also beautiful. Their **feathers** can shine with green and blue, and their long tails make them easy to spot. Whether you see them as lucky or loud, there's no doubt magpies are one of the most interesting birds around.

## Key Points
- Magpies are some of the smartest and most fascinating birds in the world.
- Magpies are symbols of good luck in some countries and bad luck in others.

## Usage Tip
### Tip 1
If so, chances are (that)...

本句型表「如果是這樣的話，那麼很有可能⋯⋯」，是根據某條件做的進一步推測，指出某件事可能為真。副詞 so 用以表示前述狀況成立，後接 that 子句引出可能發生之事，其中 that 可以省略。例句：The remote isn't working? If so, chances are (that) the batteries need replacing.

### Tip 2
There's no doubt (that)...

名詞 doubt 意為「疑慮、疑問」，用於本句型時，強調某事百分之百正確或肯定會發生，無疑之事以 that 子句引出，其中 that 可以省略。若要稍微減弱語氣，則可使用 There's little doubt (that)⋯⋯。例句：There's little doubt that AI tools have changed the way people learn and work.
