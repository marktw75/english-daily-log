# Disaster Risk Reduction (DRR)

Working together to help communities when disaster strikes

by Linda Ludwig

The risk of disasters caused by natural **hazards** is on the rise. Climate change and rapid urban development coupled with the lack of disaster readiness are transforming earthquakes, volcanic activity and tsunamis into **disastrous** events that cause a loss of thousands of dollars and lives around the world. The Asia-Pacific region is, in fact, one of the most disaster-prone areas in the world. Home to about 60 percent of the global population, this region experiences all of the above-listed events in addition to floods, typhoons and **droughts**. According to the United Nations Office for Disaster Risk Reduction (UNDRR), more than 40 percent of the world's natural disasters occur across Asia and the Pacific Ocean.

Along with its geography, a combination of climate and socioeconomic factors makes the Asia-Pacific region extremely vulnerable to disasters. Many countries lie along the Pacific Ring of Fire, a path along the Pacific Ocean that is marked by active volcanoes and earthquakes. Low-lying coastal areas and small island nations, like those of the Pacific Islands, are particularly vulnerable to rising sea levels and storm **surges**. Rapid urban development, limited infrastructure and unprotected settlements in developing areas increase the risk to life and property.

However, countries such as Japan, which now boast advanced earthquake and tsunami warning systems, have been embracing DRR strategies to **combat** risks.

Education and public awareness also play a critical role in disaster risk reduction. Countries such as the Philippines have added disaster preparation to their school **curriculum** and regularly conduct evacuation drills. In Nepal, community-based disaster risk management programs train locals to respond effectively during emergencies. These local efforts ensure that people are not only informed but also **empowered** to act quickly in times of crises.

Regional cooperation is also **imperative** for effective DRR in the Asia-Pacific area. Organizations such as the ASEAN Committee on Disaster Management (ACDM) and the Asian Disaster Preparedness Center (ADPC) promote the sharing of knowledge, joint training exercises and coordinated responses among their members.

In addition to climate change intensifying natural hazards, it is also creating more frequent and unpredictable events. Many Small Island Developing States (SIDS) and Least Developed Countries (LDC) lack the resources and infrastructure to implement comprehensive DRR measures. Politics, limited data and a lack of good **supervision** also hinder efforts in some areas.

The increasing frequency of disasters demands a strong, sustained investment in disaster risk reduction. While progress has been made through technology, education and cooperation, continued commitment is essential. From local communities to regional partnerships to worldwide cooperation, the key to a safer, more secure and sustainable future for all people is embracing the goals of DRR, **thereby** building stronger communities.

## Key Points
- The Asia-Pacific region faces disaster risks due to natural hazards, climate change and rapid growth, but some countries are improving safety through disaster risk reduction strategies.
- Education, regional cooperation and long-term investment are essential for effective disaster risk reduction in the Asia-Pacific, especially as climate change is increasing the frequency of natural disasters.

## Usage Tip
### Tip 1
The risk of + N/V-ing + is on the rise

名詞 risk 常搭配介系詞 of，再接上名詞 N 或動名詞 V-ing，表「某事會發生的風險」。片語 on the rise 則意為「在增長」，可用來說明程度、數量、價值、重要性等有正在上升的趨勢。可使用 on the decline 表減，若要表達「在下降」，則可使用 on the decline 或 in decline。例句：The risk of contracting the disease is on the rise, while vaccination rates are on the decline.

### Tip 2
Thereby + V-ing

動詞 thereby 表「因此；從而」，常見用法為「S + V, thereby + V-ing」，前面主要子句為「原因」，後面現在分詞 V-ing 為該原因導致的「結果」。在此用法中，V-ing 動作的執行者須為同方主詞，透過分詞引出結果，使表達更為簡練。該用法也常見於 thus 與 hence 這兩個詞語。例句：Cloud computing enables remote access, thereby/thus/hence increasing collaboration.
