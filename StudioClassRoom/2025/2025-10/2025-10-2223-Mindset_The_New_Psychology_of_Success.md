# Mindset: The New Psychology of Success

Change the way you think, change your future

summarized by Simon Briggs

Why do some people overcome challenges while others give up? Carol S. Dweck explores this question in *Mindset: The New Psychology of Success*. The book's main **notion** is simple but powerful: The way we think about our abilities can shape our entire lives.

According to Dweck, people tend to have either a fixed mindset or a growth mindset. Those with a fixed mindset believe that abilities can't be **altered**. They may avoid challenges or feel **discouraged** by failure. But those with a growth mindset believe they can improve with effort, and they often push themselves to grow.

Dweck explains that people with a growth mindset are more likely to have an **appreciation** for learning. They don't see failure as a sign of weakness but as a chance to improve. This kind of thinking encourages persistence. Over time, that can lead to better **outcomes** — not only in school but also in sports, relationships and work.

The book also explores strategies through which parents and teachers can help young people develop a growth mindset. Giving praise for effort and not just for talent can make a big difference. The key is helping others believe they can improve through effort — because they really can.

So how can you apply a growth mindset to your daily life? Dweck offers several practical **suggestions**. One important idea is to pay attention to your self-talk. When you fail, ask yourself how you can improve next time. When you **confront** something difficult, avoid saying, "I'm just not good at this." Instead, say, "I'm not good at this yet." That one word — yet — can change your whole attitude. It reminds you that you're still learning and growing.

Another useful strategy is to **embrace** challenges. Don't back away from something just because it seems hard. That's exactly the time to keep going. Dweck shares the stories of successful athletes and business leaders who kept pushing themselves even after setbacks. Their growth mindset helped them stay motivated. They understood that **adversity** was part of the journey — not something to fear.

Finally, the book encourages readers to view effort as a strength, not a weakness. Our culture often praises natural talent. But according to Dweck, it's effort that leads to lasting success. With this mindset, we can all become better learners, **executives** and friends. *Mindset* shows that change is not just possible — it's powerful.

## Key Points
- Mindset explains the difference between fixed and growth mindsets, and how persistence and emphasizing effort leads to better outcomes in various areas of life.
- The book offers strategies for applying a growth mindset for lasting success, such as embracing challenges, changing self-talk and valuing effort over natural talent.

## Usage Tip
### Tip 1
N + through which S + V

此處的 through which 為「介系詞 + 關係代名詞」的結構，引導形容詞子句，修飾 which 代指的 N，表「透過這 N，S 做到了某事」。該子句可直接跟在被形容名詞後，讓句子更為精簡，同時說明達成目標的方法或管道。例句：Scientists are developing new methods through which more accurate data can be collected.

### Tip 2
It + be + N + that + S + V

「It is/was ... that ...」為強調句構，能將句子特定資訊（常為名詞、介系詞片語或副詞子句）置於 be 動詞和 that 之間予以凸顯，意為「正是⋯⋯」。如本句即形容名詞 effort 具有促進成功之效，較 Effort leads to lasting success 更具強調語氣。根據時態，be 動詞可為現在式的 is 或過去式的 was。例句：It was by reducing plastic use that the community created a cleaner environment.
