# Taiwan Gets Tough on Asbestos

How Taiwan is using tech to build an asbestos-free future

by David Morton

In the past, asbestos roof tiles were used on buildings all over Taiwan. While this material was popular, affordable and abundant, experts later discovered that it was hazardous to human health. Whoever breathes in asbestos **fibers** faces a higher risk of developing lung cancer or one of several other diseases that affect the lungs. Although Taiwan officially **abolished** the use of this material on new buildings in 2006, many older buildings with asbestos roofs still remain today.

As these roofs age, they naturally **deteriorate**. Cracks or damage can allow dangerous fibers to break free and float through the air, putting those nearby at great risk, especially in crowded urban areas.

To deal with the situation, the locations of all asbestos roofs on the island needed to be accounted for, which was a difficult challenge. In the past, this work had to be done in person, which required a lot of time and effort. But Taiwan's Ministry of Environment took a major step in 2021 by introducing **innovative** tools that could monitor asbestos rooftops across the nation.

One of the most **notable** tools they used was hyperspectral imaging. This technology works by analyzing the differences in how various materials reflect wavelengths of light — much like how fingerprints are used for identification. With this tool, officials could detect asbestos roofs from **aerial** photography without needing to physically visit every building.

The technology behind hyperspectral imaging is amazing, but the asbestos project didn't rely on that one method. Instead, it **compiled** data from four different types of images: satellite photos, aerial photography, drone aerial photography and Google street views in addition to hyperspectral data. Using AI and machine learning to process the data, the government then built a **comprehensive** database, which showed the exact locations of asbestos roofs throughout Taiwan. This system allowed the government to efficiently map over 230,000 asbestos roofs across the country. With this detailed information, they could **allocate** workers and resources to efficiently demolish or renovate buildings that contained asbestos. It also gave local governments access to accurate data that would help guide their removal plans.

In 2023, the Ministry of Environment launched Taiwan's largest asbestos removal project **to date**. They successfully secured NT$1.6 billion in funding from the Executive Yuan and provided **subsidies** to local authorities to help cover the cost of removing asbestos roofs. This smart system is not only helping officials protect public health, it is also setting an example for other nations that may be struggling with the problem of asbestos. With **cutting-edge** technologies like AI, machine learning and hyperspectral imaging in hand, Taiwan has become a leader in solving this difficult environmental problem. In the future, Taiwan plans to continue using remote sensing technology to monitor asbestos roofs and ensure that more and more buildings are free of dangerous materials.

What Taiwan has done with this comprehensive project is take a big step toward a safe, healthy and asbestos-free future.

## Key Points
- Although Taiwan officially abolished the use of asbestos in 2006, when these buildings deteriorate, they release hazardous fibers into the air.
- The government uses innovative technologies to compile different image data, locate the presence of asbestos and allocate workers to efficiently demolish or renovate the affected buildings.

## Usage Tip
### Tip 1
Whoever/Whatever/Whichever + V...

這些複合關係代名詞由 who, what, which 加上字尾 -ever 所形成，可引導名詞子句或副詞子句。whoever 表示「無論誰」，whatever 表示「無論什麼」，whichever 用於有限定選項時作為「無論哪一個」。例句：Whichever team arrives first will get a free gift.

### Tip 2
V-ing/p.p. ..., S + V

此句型為使用現在分詞 V-ing 或過去分詞 p.p. 的分詞構句用法，用來針對主要子句 (S + V) 提供背景說明、條件、時間或原因等資訊，使語句更加精簡且內容豐富。一般而言，V-ing 現在分詞開頭表示主動，p.p. 過去分詞開頭表示被動或已完成的動作。例句：Surrounded by reporters, the actor quickly left the building.
