# Discover the Charm of Chiang Mai

To truly experience authentic Thai culture, head to this spectacular northern city

by Pamela Osment

Chiang Mai, Thailand, a beautiful **gem** located among green mountains and tropical forests, offers adventures of all kinds. Thailand's second-largest city successfully blends history, culture, ancient traditions and modern attractions.

Unlike the **hustle and bustle** of Bangkok, Chiang Mai is more **laid-back** and less expensive. These are just two of the reasons that millions of visitors flock to this northern city on an **annual** basis. Once there, it's hard to know where to start, but shopping is always a great place to begin.

**Boasting** many lively markets, Chiang Mai is a shopper's paradise! The city's largest market, the Tha Phae Walking Street market is **massive**. It takes place every Sunday night. You'll find everything from **handicrafts** and clothes to Thai food like coconut pancakes, rolled ice cream, mango sticky rice and more.

If you're a coffee lover or **foodie**, the city will delight you. With hundreds of coffee shops, Chiang Mai has become widely known for its coffee culture. The northern city has also become famous for its food tours, including A *Chef's* Tour with over 16 different samplings. Other food tours take you to local markets, where you'll taste and learn about a variety of northern Thai food.

For something unique, visit the hill tribes or plan an elephant **encounter**. The people of Thailand have an enduring connection with elephants. Through the years, elephants have **accompanied** them in battle, helped with work, provided transportation and so much more. Today, there's a great emphasis on honoring and providing ***ethical*** care for these gentle giants. Located in and around Chiang Mai, elephant sanctuaries allow visitors to participate in the animals' daily activities: preparing their food, feeding, exercising and bathing them.

Close to Chiang Mai live several hill tribes, including the famous Karen (Long Neck Tribe). This tribe's women are known for wearing brass rings around their necks, which are thought to **lengthen** their necks, but **in reality**, they don't. Visiting these villages allows you to learn more about their cultures through their crafts, languages and colorful clothes.

On November 5th and 6th, Chiang Mai's night skies will be illuminated by thousands of **shimmery** lanterns. The **jaw-dropping** spectacle is the highlight of the annual Yi Peng Lantern Festival, which celebrates the rainy season's end. Before the lantern launch, the city celebrates with parades, performances and fireworks.

If you plan to visit Thailand and want more than a beach vacation, check out Chiang Mai.

## Key Points
- Chiang Mai, Thailand's second largest city, has a much more relaxed atmosphere than Bangkok.
- Chiang Mai offers visitors encounters through hill tribe villages and the stunning Yi Peng Lantern Festival, as well as an ethical elephant's unique cultural experience.

## Usage Tip
### Tip 1
V-ing, S + V

現在分詞 V-ing 引導分詞構句，修飾主要子句的主詞，能為其提供背景、原因、狀態等細節補充。使用 V-ing 表示該動作是由主詞「主動」做出；若是要表達主詞「被動」接受某動作，則使用過去分詞 p.p. 來引導分詞構句。例句：Wrapped in paper, the vase is protected from damage.

### Tip 2
S + be + thought/said/believed + to VR/have + p.p.

本句型用於間接轉述，表達「S 被認為／猜設／被相信（做了某事）」。所做的事若使用 to VR 來表示，代表該動作與 be 動詞同時發生；若使用 to have p.p.，則代表該動作早於 be 動詞所指時間。本句型亦能以虛主詞 it 改寫，即 It + be + thought/said/believed that S + V。例句：The athlete is said to have used performance-enhancing drugs.
