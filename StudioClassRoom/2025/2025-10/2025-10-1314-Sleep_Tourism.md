# Sleep Tourism

Take the vacation of your dreams

by Linda Ludwig

When you think about vacations, what comes to mind? Taking a fast-paced tour through 10 countries in a week? Hiking across the mountains of Switzerland? Visiting family or friends and hanging out with them all hours of the day and night? In general, sleep has not been viewed as a **priority** for people on vacation — until now. There's a new trend in town — sleep tourism. What it all comes down to is people's desire to hit the hay and focus on rest and **recovery**.

People's lives are filled with deadlines, demands and stress, so getting a good night's sleep has become a luxury. Australia reports that more than one-third of adults there don't get the recommended amount of sleep. The United Kingdom reports that 74 percent of its adults describe sleeping poorly. Additionally, the U.S. has **revealed** that 36 percent of Americans aren't getting enough sleep.

Hilton Hotels' 2024 Trends Report stated that the top reason people wanted to travel last year was to rest. The report further stated that some travelers are building their entire vacation around resting and recharging.

Hotels and vacation places worldwide have jumped on the sleep bandwagon. Wanting to help their guests catch some Zs, some hotels **furnish** a "pillow menu." Upon arriving, guests can choose from a selection of pillows that range from down to memory foam. And that's just the beginning of what this $700 billion business presents. Sleep tourism offers everything from week-long retreats to short, **relaxing** breaks with sleep-friendly spa treatments. Some places even offer people opportunities to have medical issues diagnosed and treated. In Spain, one wellness **clinic** employs a sleep medical doctor who creates a "Sleep Well Package" for people experiencing sleep problems.

One Italian resort uses traditional Chinese medicine treatments to improve people's sleep. At an Arizona spa, guests can participate in activities that promote **restoring** sleep. A place in Thailand offers a special massage followed by a relaxing bath to help people drift off to sleep. Guests at a Colombia hotel can fall asleep to a "sound bath" provided by a live musician.

These days, the best way for some to take a vacation is to sleep through it.

## Key Points
- More people are now taking vacations to relax, making rest the main focus of their travel.
- Many places around the world are now offering services and treatments to help guests sleep better.

## Usage Tip
### Tip 1
What it all comes down to is + N/that-clause

片語 come down to 意為「歸結為⋯⋯，最終取決於⋯⋯」，本句型使用 what 引導名詞子句當主詞，說明某件事情最終歸結到底的核心原因。其核心以名詞 N 或 that 名詞子句的形式呈現，與句前方 what 所引導的名詞子句達到句構上的平衡。例句：What it all comes down to is that time is limited, so we have to manage it well.

### Tip 2
Upon + V-ing, S + V

此用法中的 upon 表「⋯⋯就⋯⋯」，說明一個動作發生後，另一個動作隨即發生，意近於 as soon as，但 as soon as 後面承接子句，upon 則因是介系詞，後方搭配名詞性 V-ing。請注意，V-ing 動作的執行者必須與主要子句之主詞相同。例句：Upon recovering from jet lag, she explored the city with excitement.
