# Happy Birthday, Johann Strauss!

Celebrating 200 years of the "Waltz King"

by Pamela Osment

This year, Vienna, Austria, is shining a **spotlight** on one of its greatest stars — Johann Strauss II — in celebration of his 200th birthday. Strauss's music once filled the ballrooms of Vienna and the world, and his enduring classics will do so again throughout 2025 as the city and the world pay **tribute** to this **renowned** musician.

Born in Vienna on October 25, 1825, Johann Strauss II, best known as the "Waltz King," had music in his blood. Johann's father, Johann Strauss I, was a celebrated composer and conductor in his own right. Though he could see from an early age that his son loved music and hoped to follow in the elder Strauss's footsteps, he was opposed to that path. He feared that his son would never live up to his own success and would bring embarrassment to the family. Johann the son, however, wasn't able to deny his own passion for music, secretly taking violin lessons, learning musical composition and **furtively** writing some early works while still a teenager.

A public rivalry developed between father and son when, in 1844, the younger Johann founded his own orchestra. When Strauss I passed away five years later, Johann **unified** their two orchestras and began an international tour of Russia and England, gaining enormous popularity.

With the freedom to follow his dreams, Johann the son's **productivity** increased by leaps and bounds as the young man composed scores of musical pieces. By the time of his death in 1899, Johann II had written over 500 musical works, most of which were waltzes.

Although the waltz already existed, Johann Strauss II is credited with **refining** it and adding elegance to the genre. He is largely responsible for its tremendous popularity in the 19th century.

Strauss's *The Blue Danube* and many other pieces helped make the Viennese waltz a social phenomenon in Europe's most popular ballrooms. In more recent years, Strauss's **masterpiece**, *The Blue Danube*, has entered into pop culture, making its way into numerous movies, TV shows, commercials and video games.

During his lifetime, Strauss became a **bona fide** celebrity all around the world, but nowhere more so than in his own beloved Vienna. This year, the beautiful city will host everything from candlelit waltzes to costumed balls. There will be concerts, dramas, digital art and more. Visitors can tour Strauss's apartment and visit his tomb, as well as many of his favorite places.

Vienna's 2025 **motto** is "King of Waltz, Queen of Music," with the Waltz King's queen being Vienna. Two hundred years later, Strauss's music, which still waltzes on in the city of its birth, continues to charm people everywhere.

## Key Points
- This year, Vienna, Austria is celebrating the 200th birthday of Johann Strauss II, the "Waltz King," who followed his passion for music and became a world-famous musician.
- During his lifetime, Strauss wrote over 500 musical works, perhaps the most recognizable of which is "The Blue Danube."

## Usage Tip
### Tip 1
S + feared that + S + would + V

動詞 fear 意為「擔憂、害怕」，後面可接 that 子句表憂心之事，子句中常使用助動詞 will 或 would 來表某事「未來可能會發生」，也能以 may/might have p.p. 表示某事「可能已經發生」。若不具體指出是誰在擔心，或某事是一個普遍的擔憂，fear 亦可搭配虛主詞 it，形成句型 It is/was feared that ...。例句：It is feared that the hikers may have been trapped by the landslide.

### Tip 2
nowhere more so than + (place)

本句型為「沒有其他地方比（某處）更如此」，強調某處是最能觀察到某狀態的地方。通過否定字詞 nowhere 加上比較級，本句型能增強語氣，傳達最高級的隱含語義，其中 so 是用來指涉前面提起的事件。例句：Bubble tea shops can be found around the world, but nowhere more so than in Taiwan.
